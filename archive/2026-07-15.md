## 5. Desk Angle — flows, vol, positioning

- **The pain trade was up, and it paid.** A market braced for an oil-driven inflation scare got a downside CPI shock instead; the resulting squeeze (KOSPI +7%, Nasdaq +0.9%, semis leading) suggests positioning was defensive/short into the print. That leaves the tape vulnerable to a *continuation* chase — but also to a sharp reversal if ASML disappoints or a Hormuz enforcement headline hits.
- **Banks: lean into the read-through, but be selective.** GS/JPM's equities-trading blowout (+72%/+86%) is most relevant to the *markets-levered* European IBs — **Deutsche Bank and Barclays** (FICC/equities), **UBS** (wealth + markets), **BNP/SocGen**. The pure-retail/domestic banks share less of it. This is a stock-selection, not a blanket-sector, signal — and much of the good news may open priced.
- **Semis: respect the two-way gap risk.** ASML options are pricing a large swing (~8%). The KOSPI squeeze skews sentiment positive, but with bookings undisclosed the reaction is a *tone* trade — a guide-in-line quarter into a +65% YTD stock is not obviously a buy. Defined-risk (straddle/strangle already rich; consider directional call spreads only with conviction) beats naked exposure.
- **Energy: the cleanest positive-carry hedge.** Long European integrated oils (Shell, Total) keeps you paid on the Hormuz tail while the soft-CPI backdrop supports the broader cyclical bid. The risk is a diplomacy headline (revived 14-point framework) that gaps oil back.
- **Vol:** US VIX was contained (~16–17) even through the conflict; the soft CPI likely presses it lower. European V2X similarly subdued. That makes *owning* optionality around today's binary events (ASML, PPI) relatively cheap versus the potential move.
- **Where risk is skewed:** constructive into the open (soft CPI + bank blowout + KOSPI squeeze), but the two identifiable landmines are (i) an ASML tone-miss dragging EU tech, and (ii) a Hormuz enforcement/tanker escalation spiking oil and reviving the inflation fear Warsh is warning about.
