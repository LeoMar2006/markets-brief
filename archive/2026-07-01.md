## 3. What's Moving Markets

**1) The AI/chip melt-up capped a record H1 — but breadth is the question.** A record chip rally added ~$2tn of combined market cap to Micron/Intel/AMD in Q2 alone; NVIDIA order flow and Micron's HBM guide keep the *demand* leg intact. The bear case was never demand — it's return-on-capex and valuation. With BTC and ETH refusing to confirm and the rally narrow, this reads as a powerful-but-concentrated move, not a broadening all-clear. Respect the trend; don't mistake it for consensus.

**2) Yen at 40-year lows — intervention is a live tail.** USD/JPY ~161.95 sits within ~5 pips of the ~162 MoF trigger. Japan has spent ~¥11.7tn YTD to little lasting effect against a ~275bp rate gap. The nuance: pressure is building into a *risk-on* tape, which is less dangerous than intervention into a selloff. But a MoF move overlapping any fresh AI wobble is the classic 300–500-pip air-pocket setup. Warsh/NFP-driven USD strength is the most likely route to 162.

**3) Euro-area flash CPI (10:00) into a euro at 1-year lows.** Consensus ~3.0% headline (from 3.2%), core ~2.5%. The ECB has effectively signalled June's hike was the last (deposit 2.25%). A soft print reinforces the on-hold stance and could pressure EUR/USD below 1.14; a sticky services number is the upside surprise risk. Sintra ECB commentary is the wildcard overlay.

**4) Copper — the 232 refined-copper report has landed; the decision hasn't.** Commerce (Lutnick) was due to deliver its domestic-market update to the President by 30 Jun. The framework on the table: a **phased universal duty on refined copper — 15% from Jan-2027, rising to 30% from Jan-2028.** *Note the nuance:* public sources confirm the report was due/delivered but **do not yet confirm a signed Presidential proclamation** — a formal decision appears forthcoming. Markets treat the phased-15% as the contained base case; Goldman flags copper could breach **$14,000/t** if duties proceed. COMEX inventories at a record ~652kt show how much has already been pulled forward. Soft/no action = disappointment risk for the front end.

**5) Oil's Q2 collapse reframes the macro.** Brent ~$73 after a ~30% quarterly drop — Hormuz reopened, Gulf-trapped barrels and Iran waiver volumes hit a well-supplied market. This is a *disinflationary tailwind* that cuts against the hawkish-Fed narrative on the margin, and it's why the geopolitical de-escalation matters beyond the headlines. A genuine Hormuz re-closure remains an un-priced asymmetric tail, but the market has repeatedly shrugged off flare-ups.

**6) Gold's worst month since 2008.** –12.7% in June, back below $4,000. Higher-for-longer, a firmer USD and risk-on bleeding the haven bid have hit a crowded long hard. JPM's longer-term ~$6,000 Q4 call now looks stretched near-term. A hot NFP Thursday could press the position further.

**7) UK leadership transition — orderly.** Post-Starmer, Labour nominations open 9 Jul / close 16 Jul, leader by ~29 Aug. Burnham remains the clear frontrunner (near-coronation; no declared challenger). Gilts (~4.75%) and GBP (~1.34) have taken it in stride; the deferred fiscal question is a term-premium risk for later, not now.

- **Positioning is long and narrow.** The H1 melt-up is concentrated in semis/AI mega-cap. That's a momentum-chaser's dream and a gap-risk nightmare — a narrow leadership tape with crypto *not* confirming is vulnerable to a fast unwind on any AI-monetisation or capex-guide scare. Chase with a stop, not conviction.

- **Rates skew hawkish.** JOLTS resilience + hawkish-hold dots + firm USD argue the pain trade is *higher* front-end yields, not lower. A dovish Warsh surprise is the low-probability, high-impact risk to that.
- **JPY is the cleanest expression of tail risk.** Long-vol/optionality on USD/JPY around 162 captures the intervention air-pocket without needing to call direction.
- **Euro downside momentum** into CPI + Sintra; EUR/USD sub-1.14 opens on a soft print.
- **Gold**: crowded long unwinding; don't catch the knife until $3,950–4,000 base holds and real yields turn.
