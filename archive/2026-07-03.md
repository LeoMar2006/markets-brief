## 1. Overnight Market Wrap

**The crack finally came from the data, not the tape.** June payrolls printed **+57k vs +115k consensus** — the softest headline in over a year — with April/May revised down a combined **74k** and the unemployment "improvement" to 4.2% driven by a **0.3pp drop in participation to 61.5%** (lowest since March 2021), i.e. a soft print dressed as a firm one. That did the work Tokyo's ¥11.7tn couldn't: it took the dollar off its 15-month high, pulled the yen back from the brink, and revived rate-cut hope across every asset that had been pinned by higher-for-longer.

But underneath, the more important move was **rotation, not relief**: the **Dow ripped to a fresh record (+1.03%, 52,844)** on cyclicals/value while **mega-cap AI/tech gave back ground** (Nasdaq lower, S&P roughly flat). One day's tape, but it's the first session in this melt-up where breadth broadened *away* from the chips — worth respecting, not yet a regime change.

## 3. What's Moving Markets

**1. Payrolls cracked — the first real dent in higher-for-longer.** +57k vs +115k, with 74k of downward revisions and participation sliding to a 2021 low. The internals were soft too: leisure/hospitality −61k on weak seasonal hiring, gains concentrated in professional/business services (+36k), health care (+22k) and social assistance (+25k). This is the "first crack" the desk had been watching for since ADP (+98k) undershot on 1 Jul. It doesn't force a cut — but it makes the July/September *hike* the dots implied much harder to justify, and that repricing rippled everywhere.

**2. The rotation is the story, not the relief.** Soft data + revived cut hope would normally lift the whole tape; instead the **Dow hit a record while the Nasdaq fell.** Money rotated out of the crowded, narrow AI/semis complex into rate-sensitive cyclicals and value. That's either (a) a healthy broadening that the melt-up needs to survive, or (b) a one-session unwind of a stretched long. Treat it as a tell to monitor, not a confirmed regime shift — but note the concentration risk in mega-cap tech is exactly what a data-driven rate repricing punishes first.

**3. Yen: soft NFP did what ¥11.7tn couldn't.** USD/JPY fell ~1% back to **~161.0–161.5**, below the 162 line the MoF treats as a trigger, purely on dollar softness — no confirmed intervention required. FinMin Katayama kept up the verbal warnings into the thin US holiday, but the acute intervention tail has eased. The carry is still alive (~250bp gap) and thin Friday liquidity keeps a residual air-pocket risk, but the pressure valve released itself.

**4. Gold's broken long gets a bid back.** Gold snapped **+2% to ~$4,130**, back above $4,100, after a −12.7% June. The single soft data point revived the rate-cut/real-yield case that had been crushing it. One bounce doesn't repair a broken structure, but the reflex was textbook: gold is the cleanest long expression of "the Fed can't hike into a softening labour market." September hike odds fell to ~45–51% from ~66% pre-data.

**5. Crypto finally twitches — but on rate beta, not breadth.** BTC briefly topped **$62k** (first reclaim of $60k+ in weeks); ETH broke **$1,700**. ~$460m of shorts were liquidated. This looks less like a breadth confirmation of the equity melt-up and more like high-beta participation in the same rate-cut repricing that lifted gold and the front end. Watch whether BTC *holds* $60k+ — that would be the genuine risk-appetite tell; a fade back under would keep the negative-divergence flag intact.

**6. Brent below $71 — lowest since February.** Oil kept sliding (−0.83% to **$70.57**) as Hormuz shipping normalised and US-Iran indirect talks showed progress. A well-supplied market and a disinflationary tailwind that quietly cuts *against* the hawkish-Fed story — and now reinforces the soft-NFP cut narrative. Re-closure of the Strait remains the un-priced asymmetric tail.

**7. Copper — refined-232 still unsigned.** The Commerce report to the President was due 30 Jun; the recommended framework is a phased universal refined duty (15% Jan-27 → 30% Jan-28). **No signed proclamation has surfaced.** COMEX sits ~$6.10/lb with record inventories — the disappointment air-pocket the desk flagged if the decision slips. Goldman still pegs >$14,000/t if duties proceed; a surprise signature snaps it violently higher.

**8. Euro-area disinflation holds; EUR bounces on the dollar.** June flash HICP at 2.8% (from 3.2%) keeps the ECB done-hiking narrative intact. EUR/USD rallied off its 1-year low toward **~1.152** — but that's dollar weakness, not euro strength. The ECB's job is finished; the pair is now a pure USD-rates story.

**9. Tariff-legal overhang building into late July.** Section 122's 10% surcharge expires **24 Jul**; the CIT ruled against it (under appeal, tariffs still collected) after SCOTUS struck the IEEPA "Liberation Day" tariffs in February. A term-premium and trade-policy tail worth pre-positioning as the deadline nears.

## 4. Desk Angle — Flows, Vol, Positioning

- **The pain trade reversed, and positioning amplified it.** Consensus was long-USD, short-JPY, short-gold, underweight-crypto, long-narrow-AI. One soft number hit *every* crowded book at once — which is exactly why the moves were outsized relative to a 58k miss. When positioning is this one-way, the data doesn't need to be dramatic to force the unwind.
- **Rotation risk > melt-up risk today.** The Dow-record / Nasdaq-down split is the flow signal that matters. If real money is broadening out of mega-cap tech into cyclicals on a softening-growth-but-easing-Fed setup, the narrow-long semis complex is the vulnerable book — not because AI is over, but because it's *crowded* and it's the first thing sold when the rate story shifts.
- **FX: the intervention tail deflated, but don't fully unwind the convexity.** USD/JPY back below 162 removes the immediate MoF trigger, and soft NFP caps the dollar. But it's a US-holiday Friday — thin liquidity still argues for keeping *some* cheap JPY convexity on rather than selling all vol into the gap. The asymmetry shifted from "intervention air-pocket up in USD/JPY" toward "dollar grinds lower if the data trend continues."
- **Rates: bull-steepening bias.** Front end led the move (2y −5bp to 4.108%) as the hike premium bled out; 10y barely moved. FOMC minutes Wed 9 Jul are the next test of whether the front end can extend — a hawkish-toned minutes would fade this; a balanced one lets 2s keep falling.
- **Vol:** event-vol into NFP unwound fast; today's holiday means low realised but gappy conditions. Skew still favours owning cheap downside on the concentrated semis complex over the thin session, and modest JPY convexity.
- **Commodities skew flipped for gold, unchanged for copper.** Gold's pain-trade-lower thesis is now two-sided after the +2% snap — the soft-data bid is real. Copper's air-pocket persists while the 232 signature is missing; Brent's disinflationary drift continues to work *with* the new soft-labour narrative.

## 6. Trade Ideas *(illustrative, not advice)*

1. **Lean into the rotation, hedged.** Long cyclicals/value vs. short crowded mega-cap semis (e.g. Dow/RTY vs. Nasdaq-100). *Thesis:* soft-growth-but-easing-Fed favours broadening; the narrow AI long is the vulnerable book. *Risk:* one session isn't a trend — the melt-up can simply reclaim the chips.
2. **Front-end receiver / 2s steepener.** Position for the 2y to keep bleeding the hike premium as the labour crack develops. *Thesis:* July/September hike is now hard to justify; front end leads. *Risk:* hawkish FOMC minutes (9 Jul) or a hot CPI reasserts higher-for-longer.
3. **Fade residual USD/JPY strength, keep cheap JPY convexity.** *Thesis:* soft NFP capped the dollar and released the intervention pressure below 162; trend now favours a lower pair. *Risk:* carry + thin holiday liquidity can still gap higher; size small.
4. **Gold — stop pressing the short.** The −12.7% June trade is done as a one-way pain lower; the +2% snap says soft data revives the bid. *Thesis:* two-sided from here, upside optionality on further labour softening. *Risk:* a hawkish minutes / firm CPI re-breaks it toward $3,950.
