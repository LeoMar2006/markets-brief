## 3. What's Moving Markets

1. **US–Iran deal confirmed — the 2026 macro pivot.** Trump: agreement "now complete"; blockade lifted, Hormuz to reopen, signing Friday 19 Jun in Switzerland (Trump electronically or VP Vance in person). Hormuz handled ~20% of global oil/LNG pre-war. *Caveats to respect:* Iran is publicly pushing a **differing version** of the terms; the nuclear file and other hard issues are kicked to a **60-day negotiation window** — i.e. this is a framework/MOU, not a final treaty, so reversibility risk is live. Israeli strikes in Lebanon over the weekend are a wildcard that could test the agreement.

2. **Oil's two-speed reality.** Price says "supply's coming back" (Brent low-$80s); logistics say "not for months" (mine-clearance, vessel backlog). Expect a steeper backwardation-to-contango debate and headline-driven chop. Energy equities lagging the index move is consistent with that.

3. **BoJ Tuesday — the hike isn't the story, the guidance is.** A move to 1.0% (highest since 1995) is fully priced. The market reaction hinges on whether the BoJ signals an accelerated path (BofA pencils in further hikes Oct-26/Mar-27/Jul-27 to 1.75%). Aggressive guidance + USDJPY already at 160 = carry-unwind and JPY-liquidity-drain risk for global risk assets.

4. **Warsh's first FOMC (Wed).** Hold at 3.50–3.75% is ~98–99%. The event is the **dot plot and tone**: consensus is an explicit shift **from an easing bias to neutral**. Risk is hawkish — higher dots tighten financial conditions without a hike and would hit the long end, gold and the front-end (SOFR/2y). A dot-plot redesign/abolition remains a live rumour.

5. **SpaceX (SPCX) — the $75bn capital sponge.** Closed Friday debut at **$161.11 (+19.3%)** off the $135 IPO; intraday high $176.52; market cap **>$2tn**, now the 6th-largest US public company. Avg 12m target ~$164 (range $63–$227 — i.e. wide disagreement). Largest IPO ever; the capital absorbed may quietly cap marginal risk appetite elsewhere. Watch week-two price action and S&P inclusion chatter (profitability hurdle not met).

6. **Gold's quiet defiance.** Holding ~$4,150–4,200 despite the risk-premium unwind tells you the bid is structural (central banks, real-rate cap), not just fear. Wednesday's dots are the near-term binary for a crowded long.

7. **Copper's structural deficit intact.** $6.43/lb; Goldman flags a ~330kt 2026 deficit (AI data-centres, EV, grid) even as it sees prices easing from records. Improved EM-growth optics from the deal are an incremental tailwind.

8. **Crypto's non-confirmation.** BTC ~$62k / ETH ~$1.7k failing to join the equity rally is the cleanest "positioning is fragile" signal on the board into a BoJ hike.

## 4. Desk Angle — Flows, Vol, Positioning

- **Skew of risk:** The deal is a genuine de-risking event, but **a lot is now in the price** after a multi-week rally and a Monday Asia melt-up. The asymmetry from here is less "deal upside" and more "execution/central-bank downside." Buy-the-rumour is largely done; "sell-the-(Friday)-signing" into a US holiday is a real setup.
- **Vol:** Expect oil and rate vol to stay elevated through the CB trio even as equity vol compresses on the deal. Cross-asset correlation is high right now (everything-rallies); that breaks the moment one central bank surprises hawkish.
- **Positioning pressure points:** (1) **USDJPY at 160** — long-carry into a BoJ hike is the most crowded, most fragile trade; an aggressive BoJ + MoF intervention combo would force a fast unwind that drags EM and equities. (2) **Crowded gold longs** into Wednesday's dots. (3) **$75bn SpaceX absorption** thinning liquidity for marginal longs. (4) **Juneteenth Friday** = thin books into the signing headline.
- **Net read:** lean constructive but not chase; the cleaner expression is relative-value and fading extremes (oil downside, JPY weakness) rather than naked beta into three central banks in three days.
