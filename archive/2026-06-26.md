## TOP LINE

**The AI trade is being repriced — and this time it has a fundamental catalyst, not just a positioning excuse.** Two days after Micron's blowout "demand-is-not-the-problem" print, the equity narrative has flipped hard to *who pays for the AI capex and when does it earn a return*. Overnight: an NYT/Bloomberg report that **OpenAI is leaning toward delaying its IPO to 2027** — unable to secure demand at Sam Altman's insisted **$1tn valuation**, against a freshly disclosed **$38.5bn net loss** on ~$34bn of compute/R&D spend — lit a fuse under Asian tech. **SoftBank −13%**, **Kospi −8% (20-min circuit breaker), SK Hynix ~−10%, Samsung −7%, Nikkei −5.1% back below 69,000**, broad Asia −3.2%. US futures soft: **S&P −0.8%, Nasdaq-100 −1.6%**. The Nasdaq has now fallen four straight sessions. Yesterday's US tape had already cracked — **Apple −6.1%, Microsoft −3.2%** after both flagged consumer-hardware price hikes (iPhone, Xbox), the first visible sign of AI/cost pass-through to the consumer.

This is no longer the 23 Jun "positioning flush." The demand leg (Micron: HBM sold out into 2027) is intact; what's broken is the **monetisation/return-on-capex** leg. Treat rallies as suspect until breadth and the megacap complex stabilise.

## 3. What's Moving Markets

1. **OpenAI IPO delay → AI-monetisation scare (THE story).** NYT/Bloomberg: OpenAI leaning to 2027, can't fill a $1tn book; $38.5bn FY net loss on ~$34bn compute. The market read-through: if the flagship AI franchise can't be priced and is bleeding cash, the entire data-centre capex chain (memory, GPUs, power, hyperscaler spend) faces a "where's the return" discount. SoftBank — OpenAI's marquee backer — took the brunt at −13%.

2. **Apple/Microsoft pass AI costs to the consumer.** Both announced hardware price hikes (iPhone, Xbox) on 25 Jun; Apple −6.1%, MSFT −3.2%. Tactically a margin-defence positive, but the market read it as confirmation that AI infrastructure costs are now large enough to dent the consumer — i.e., demand risk, not just capex risk.

3. **Memory complex round-trips its Micron rip.** SK Hynix −10%, Samsung −7% overnight fully unwound the 25 Jun melt-up. The Micron fundamentals (record GM ~85%, HBM sold out into 2027, $50bn Q4 guide) haven't changed — but in a capex-doubt tape, "sold out" reads as "someone is over-ordering" rather than "demand is bottomless."

4. **May PCE: hot in level, calm in surprise.** Core +0.3% m/m / **3.4% y/y** (highest since Oct-23, in-line); headline **4.1% y/y** (highest since Apr-23). Because it matched consensus and didn't *accelerate* beyond fears, the dollar slipped marginally and yields eased — a relief-by-non-disaster, not a dovish pivot. Sept hike odds ~63% (from 68%). Note: backward-looking May data, and it embeds the now-fading Hormuz energy shock.

5. **Gold's relief bounce.** Round-tripped back above $4,000 after dipping to ~$3,977 — the in-line PCE removed the immediate "faster Fed" tail and the risk-off helped. The crowded-long thesis is still live; this looks like a line-in-the-sand defence at $4k, not a trend change. Silver remains the weak link (−5.4%, G/S ~67).

6. **Crypto won't confirm anything.** BTC sub-$60k (multi-year low), ETH ~$1,560. The cleanest tell on the board: the asset that was *supposedly* losing capital to AI equities is now selling off alongside them. Broad de-risking, ETF outflows, CLARITY-Act delay overhang.

7. **UK succession premium parked in gilts.** Burnham the clear frontrunner (won Makerfield ~55%); market fears a left tack — £40bn extra borrowing for housing/infra, mansion-tax talk. Gilt 10y 4.71% (off ~4.85% highs as global duty rallied) still carries the premium; nominations open 9 Jul, close 16 Jul, new PM by 1 Sep.

8. **Brent keeps bleeding the war premium.** $74.43, −1.1%, low-$70s. Underpins the ECB "done" disinflation story and quietly undercuts the hawkish-Fed oil narrative that the June dots leaned on.

## 4. Desk Angle — Flows, Vol, Positioning

- **Where risk is skewed:** Crowded into the long-AI/long-megacap/long-USD complex going into this. The pain trade remains *down* in tech and *a squeeze* in the underweights (defensives, value, non-US). After four down Nasdaq sessions and an 8% Kospi halt, some capitulation is building — but the OpenAI catalyst is fundamental, so don't reflexively fade the first bounce.
- **Vol:** expect a bid in index/single-name vol (chips, semis, SoftBank ADRs); skew steepening to the downside. The risk into US hours is a **gamma-driven air-pocket** if the cash open confirms futures, set against **quarter-end rebalancing** that could mechanically buy the dip — two opposing forces meeting on the same day. Two-way, fast.
- **Rates/FX:** front-end anchored (hike-bias intact, PCE in-line) so rate-cut hopes can't cushion equities — a key difference from past growth scares. USD softness on PCE is marginal; **USD/JPY ~161.3 keeps MoF/BoJ intervention a live overnight tail** — a surprise here would amplify any carry unwind into the tech selloff.
- **Cross-asset:** gold as the de-risking hedge is behaving; copper insulated by its own (Section 232) catalyst; crypto giving no signal except "risk-off."
- **Net:** lean defensively into the open, respect quarter-end mechanics for a possible head-fake rally, and watch megacap breadth and SK Hynix/Samsung as the real-time barometer of whether the capex-doubt is spreading or stabilising.
