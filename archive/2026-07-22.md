1. **Semis-equipment decouples from the memory bear.** ASML +4.7%, ASMI +5.4% led STOXX tech +2.1% — the "equipment fundamentals ≠ memory positioning" trade playing out ahead of **BESI tomorrow** (guided rev +30–40% q/q, GM 64–66%, orders +104.5% y/y). A clean BESI beat validates the decouple; a cautious tone re-fuses the whole complex.

2. **Oil two-way on a live 10-day ceasefire proposal.** Iran confirms it received mediation proposals; **the White House has not accepted.** Brent held ~$89 as escalation (fresh strikes) offset diplomacy. Equities are looking through it — a fully-enforced Hormuz disruption remains un-priced (the tail), and a genuine de-escalation headline is the pain trade for oil longs and a squeeze-up for semis/travel.

3. **ECB tomorrow — hold at 2.25%, but a tightening bias.** ~88% priced to hold after June's hike; ~70% of economists see one more +25bp in September. This is a hawkish-hold regime, not a cutting cycle — NII-supportive for banks, a headwind for duration. Bund 2y >2.80% says the market believes it.

4. **UK CPI this morning.** A soft 2.6% print (from 2.8%) eases some BoE pressure and could firm gilts; an upside surprise given oil pass-through would hit the front end and lift sterling off its ~1.338 base.

5. **Section 122 tariff cliff Friday.** The 10% global surcharge sunsets by law 24 Jul; a **Section 301 forced-labor replacement (~12.5% on 46 countries)** is expected to backfill but isn't cleanly confirmed. A Brazil-specific duty reportedly lands today. Net: don't expect landed costs to actually fall — the overhang persists, and it's a European-exporter risk factor.

6. **Copper re-prices a US duty.** COMEX +3.3%, US premium to LME back near $600/t on tightening China physicals — supportive for the miners and for the Anglo/Teck strategic rationale (China clearance the last gate).

7. **UK M&A active.** Mitie +39% on OCS Group's £3.1bn agreed bid; easyJet arb spread live into early-August PUSU deadlines.

## 5. Desk Angle — flows, vol, positioning

- **The decouple is the trade, and it's crowded fast.** Equipment (ASML/ASMI/BESI) ripped while memory stays >20% off highs. Longs here are effectively short the "positioning unwind is done" thesis — high beta, event-heavy (BESI tomorrow, ASMI 28 Jul, SK Hynix 29 Jul). Skew favours owning defined-risk upside rather than chasing cash into three consecutive prints.
- **Banks: ~21% Q2 rally = a show-me tape.** With Santander today and the heavyweights (BNP 23rd, Barclays 28th, DB/UBS 29th) into month-end, the risk is asymmetric on any NII/payout disappointment — a lot is in the price. Hawkish ECB is the structural support; single-name dispersion (GS long Swedbank / short DNB) is the cleaner expression than sector beta.
- **Rates skew hawkish into the ECB.** Bund 2y >2.80% and UST 10y 4.63% mean duration is the pain trade if oil stays bid; a hawkish Lagarde Q&A tomorrow steepens the front end further. Euro downside vol into 1.14 looks under-owned given a two-sided ECB.
- **Oil is the master switch.** A de-escalation headline is a violent risk-on for European travel/consumer and a hit to energy majors; the reverse (Hormuz enforcement) is the un-priced left tail. Cheap Brent upside optionality remains a rational hedge for equity longs.
- **Positioning:** risk appetite is measurably back (crypto firm, Hang Seng squeeze, semis leadership) — but it's a bounce running straight into CPI/ECB/earnings/tariff-cliff catalysts. Respect the calendar; this is not a set-and-forget tape.
