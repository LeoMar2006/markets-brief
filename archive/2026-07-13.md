## 5. Desk Angle — flows, vol, positioning

- **Positioning into the shock:** the Street was **long Europe and structurally constructive** (record STOXX 600 last week, JPM/UBS/HSBC upgrades, 10 weeks of positive EPS revisions). That is precisely the setup that makes a Monday oil-shock gap *painful* — the pain trade is down, into crowded longs in cyclicals/semis/luxury.
- **Vol:** expect a **gap-up in V2X / European vol** at the open; realised will jump. With a binary, headline-driven catalyst (Hormuz open/closed), **defined-risk (options) beats naked directional** — you are being asked to price a bimodal outcome. Skew will bid for downside in cyclicals and upside in energy.
- **Where risk is skewed:** **asymmetric.** The market prices *disruption, not closure.* If Hormuz is genuinely closed and enforced → oil violently higher, European cyclicals/airlines/autos violently lower, gold finally catches a bid, ECB caught between growth hit and energy-inflation. If a de-escalation headline hits → the whole move gaps back (Friday's tape showed how fast "peace-talk" hope lifts airlines/luxury). **Net: carry less directional cyclical beta; own the energy/defence hedge; use options for the tails.**
- **FX/rates cross-currents:** USD/JPY at 162 is the intervention tripwire; a carry unwind on an oil/AI shock is the transmission channel into a broader risk-off. Watch the 2y — if hawkish-Warsh pricing holds *and* oil stays bid, that's the worst combination for European multiples (higher discount rate + margin squeeze).
- **Liquidity note:** Monday post-weekend-gap opens are thin; the first 30–60 min of European cash will over-shoot. Let the auction print before chasing.

## 7. European Equity Pitches
*Illustrative and educational only — not investment advice. Sizing/risk management left to the reader.*

**1) TotalEnergies (TTE FP) — LONG (cash + upside call spread)**
- *Thesis:* Cheapest of the supermajors on FCF/EV, generous buyback, and the most direct liquid European beneficiary of a Hormuz risk premium. In a world where the market is *under-pricing* a sustained closure, this is convex to the tail we think is mispriced — and pays you to wait via the buyback if nothing happens.
- *Instrument:* Cash core + Aug/Sep call spread to add convexity to the oil-spike tail without paying full premium.
- *Catalyst (dated):* **Q2 results ~30 Jul** (buyback pace + upstream guidance); ongoing Hormuz headlines are the day-to-day driver.
- *Key risk:* A genuine US-Iran de-escalation headline gaps oil — and TTE — sharply lower; refining margins already soft.

**2) Rheinmetall (RHM GY) — LONG (cash, or call spread given valuation)**
- *Thesis:* The purest liquid European play on both the acute conflict bid *and* the structural rearmament theme (Merz €500bn fiscal, NATO spend). Escalation is an order-book and sentiment tailwind; the stock is the market's go-to hedge for exactly this tape.
- *Instrument:* Given a stretched multiple, prefer a **defined-risk call spread** into the print rather than chasing cash on a gap.
- *Catalyst (dated):* **Q2 earnings ~7 Aug**; near-term, every escalation headline. NATO/EU defence-spend commitments through the summer.
- *Key risk:* Valuation is demanding — a de-escalation unwinds the momentum bid fast; any order-timing disappointment at Q2 punishes a crowded long.

**3) easyJet (EZJ LN) — LONG (merger-arb / event, cash)**
- *Thesis:* Idiosyncratic long *inside* a sector being sold on oil. Board is "minded to recommend" **Castlelake's 690p cash bid** (~75% premium). The deal — not the jet-fuel curve — is the dominant driver of the share, giving a low-beta long that is partly insulated from today's risk-off.
- *Instrument:* Cash, sized for merger-arb (spread to 690p vs. break downside).
- *Catalyst (dated):* **PUSU deadline 3 Aug** — binding offer or walk-away. Any counter-bid is upside.
- *Key risk:* **EU/UK airline foreign-ownership approval is the real deal risk**; a break returns EZJ to fundamentals *in a high-oil environment* — a double hit. This is a genuine binary; size accordingly.

**4) Wizz Air (WIZZ LN) SHORT vs Ryanair (RYA ID) LONG — PAIR**
- *Thesis:* Express the airline fuel-shock via relative value, not outright. Wizz runs the **least-hedged fuel book** and is CEE/oil-import-sensitive; Ryanair is well-hedged, lowest-cost, and structurally the survivor. A sustained oil bid should widen the fundamental gap; the pair strips out the "de-escalation gaps all airlines up" beta.
- *Instrument:* Beta-adjusted long/short pair.
- *Catalyst (dated):* **Ryanair Q1 results ~21 Jul** (hedge position + capacity/pricing) resets the leg; Wizz trading updates.
- *Key risk:* A sharp oil reversal on de-escalation lifts the more-shorted, higher-beta Wizz *more* than Ryanair (short-squeeze risk); Ryanair-specific disappointment.
