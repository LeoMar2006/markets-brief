## 3. What's Moving Markets (Europe-first)

1. **Hormuz escalation deepens — and now there are US casualties.** Nine straight nights of US strikes, three US service members killed, four vessels intercepted in the Strait, a reimposed port blockade, and a Houthi embargo on Saudi Arabia. Brent +~30% off July lows to ~$89. **The market is pricing serious disruption but *not* a fully enforced, sustained ~20%-of-seaborne closure** — that remains the un-priced upside tail for oil (downside for risk). The two-sided catalyst: an **unaccepted 10-day-pause proposal** is on the table, so a single de-escalation headline is the pain trade (oil gaps down, semis/travel squeeze up). These episodes have whipsawed all month — size accordingly.

2. **Gold's "rates > war" tell is bending.** Spot has crept back *above* $4,000 (~$4,018–4,022) after sliding below it Friday — but it's still near a nine-month low. The hawkish-Fed/real-yield regime is *still* mostly winning, but the margin is thinning as casualties mount. A decisive break **>$4,100** would confirm geopolitics finally re-rating; until then the signal is "rates narrowly ahead."

3. **The semis flush is a *memory* bear market, not a demand downgrade.** Micron, Samsung, SK Hynix and the DRAM ETF are all **>20% off recent highs**; the complex has shed **~$1.5tn since 25 Jun**. Yet the fundamentals went the *other* way: **TSMC's 16 Jul beat-and-raise** (Q2 rev $40.2bn +34%, net +77%, GM 67.7%; FY guide lifted to **>40%**, capex to **$60–64bn**) *upgraded* the AI-capex curve. **ASML (reported 15 Jul)** beat (Q2 sales €9.3bn, bookings €5.54bn vs ~€4.44bn cons.) and raised guidance — yet fell **~7%** after CEO Fouquet said he "cannot confirm" 2026 revenue growth. Read: a crowded-long unwind dragging fundamentally-de-risked EU equipment. **BESI (Thu 23 Jul) and ASMI (Tue 28 Jul) are the decouple-tests.**

4. **Ryanair is the marquee European earnings casualty.** Shares **−5/−6%** (bottom of the STOXX 600) after Q1 (FY26) **PAT −34% to €538m**: unhedged jet-fuel roughly *doubled* on the Iran spike and average fares fell **−6%**, swamping +6% traffic growth. O'Leary flagged Mideast-dented bookings and **declined FY27 guidance**. A clean, direct read-through of the oil shock into the European consumer/airline complex.

5. **Sell-side keeps *raising* Europe despite the tape — and the earnings back it.** **J.P. Morgan lifted year-end targets** — STOXX 600 to **680** (from 630), FTSE 100 to **11,000** (from 10,300), MSCI Eurozone to 420, Euro Stoxx 50 to 6,800. **HSBC upgraded Eurozone equities to Overweight** (closing its EM Overweight), flagging **banks** in particular. Bloomberg frames Q2 as **Europe's strongest earnings growth in three years**, with analyst upgrades outpacing downgrades for a **10th straight week** and European bank stocks having booked a **~21% Q2 gain**. The strategist community is treating the chip flush + war as a positioning/valuation event, not a European fundamental downgrade — worth weighing against the risk-off price action.

6. **UK: an actual change of government — market read is "relief, then a shrug."** **Andy Burnham sworn in as PM Monday** (59th PM); **John Healey named Chancellor in a surprise move** (Reeves resigned; **Mahmood stays Home Secretary** — the widely-trailed "Mahmood Chancellor" story did *not* materialise). Cabinet also reportedly: Cooper to Foreign Secretary, Lammy Deputy PM (partial/unconfirmed). Burnham pledged to honour existing fiscal rules — enough to avoid a gilt tantrum, not enough to sustain the earlier sterling rally, which reversed into a three-day slide. The deferred fiscal question is an autumn-Budget risk, not a today risk.

7. **European M&A stays busy — telecoms leads.** The **SFR carve-up** (Bouygues ~52% of carved revenue, Iliad ~27%, Orange ~21%) would rank among Europe's biggest recent telecom deals if cleared; **VodafoneThree** reportedly bid for **TalkTalk's consumer arm**; **Vodafone Greece/PPC** weighing a >1.6m-home fibre merger; **MFE raised its ProSiebenSat.1** offer. Plus the live **easyJet** contest (see §6).

8. **ECB Thursday — the reframe matters.** With the deposit rate already at **2.25% post-June-hike** and Bund 2s at post-2024 highs, Thursday is about whether Lagarde keeps a *hike* on the table into an oil-driven inflation impulse. A hawkish hold is EUR-supportive and a headwind for rate-sensitive European growth/real estate.

## 5. Desk Angle — flows, vol, positioning

- **Where risk is skewed:** the crowded expression is **long-energy / short-duration-tech / short-travel** — all three legs are the *same* Iran trade. That means a **de-escalation headline (the 10-day pause getting accepted) is the pain trade**: oil gaps down, semis and airlines squeeze up, gold falls. Given how many times this has whipsawed in July, chasing any single leg into strength is the risk. The un-priced *upside* for oil is an *enforced, sustained* Hormuz closure; the un-priced *downside* is a durable ceasefire.
- **European vol / dispersion:** the index moves (−0.3% STOXX 600) massively understate single-name dispersion (Ryanair −6% vs Siemens Energy +2.5%; a >8-pt spread) and sector spread (Oil & Gas +1% vs Travel −0.9%). This is a **dispersion/rotation tape** — favour relative-value and pairs over outright beta. Expect the VSTOXX bid to persist while both the Gulf and the memory complex are live.
- **Semis:** **BESI (Thu 23)** and **ASMI (Tue 28)** are the asymmetric setups — a capex-levered beat into a washed-out, memory-driven bear market is the decouple thesis; a cautious guide (à la ASML's "cannot confirm 2026") confirms the de-rate. High two-way gamma.
- **Banks:** into **Santander (22)/BNP (23)/Barclays (28)/DB+UBS (29)/SocGen (30)**, HSBC's Overweight-banks call meets a still-hawkish ECB (positive for NII) — but it's a *show-me* on payouts, not a gimme. SX7P hasn't front-run it.
- **Oil-levered equities:** European majors (**Shell, BP, TotalEnergies, Equinor**) and services are the direct Hormuz beneficiaries into their Q2s (~30–31 Jul); tanker names are the second-derivative rerouting play. Ryanair is the mirror-image short of the same shock.
- **Sterling/gilts:** the Burnham/Healey handover removed tail risk but added no upside catalyst; GBP on a three-day slide, gilts near two-month-high yields into Wed UK CPI. A hot CPI + hawkish global rates = further gilt pressure.
