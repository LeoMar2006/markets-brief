## 3. What's Moving Markets

### 1. US-Iran Nuclear Deal — The Event of 2026

This is the single most important macro variable in the world right now. Trump said Sunday the deal would be signed "today." Tehran's Foreign Ministry pushed back, saying the signing "will not be tomorrow" as of Saturday. Both sides have confirmed a 14-point draft MOU brokered by Pakistani mediators, finalized June 12. The text commits Iran to: (a) never pursuing nuclear weapons and suspending enrichment in exchange for sanctions relief; (b) reopening the Strait of Hormuz within 30 days of signing; (c) a 60-day MOU framework for a fuller peace settlement. Qatari mediators were in Tehran over the weekend to finalise.

Markets have already priced a deal heavily — Brent is down ~20% from its 2026 highs. But important nuances the market hasn't fully processed: physical Hormuz clearance takes time (mines, traffic management); Iranian oil production ramp from idled fields takes months; and the 60-day MOU is a framework, not a final settlement, meaning it remains reversible. Fitch estimates Hormuz physically reopens end-July. If the deal is delayed or collapses, oil snaps back through $95 and the rates/equities complex reprices sharply — this is the biggest single tail risk heading into the week.
