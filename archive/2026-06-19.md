## 3. What's Moving Markets

**1) Iran–US deal signs in Geneva today (oil).** The headline risk event for crude resolves: a war-ending MoU mediated by Pakistan/Qatar, Hormuz reopening, US blockade lifted. Brent is sub-$78 vs the ~$108–115 April–conflict peak — roughly a 30%+ round-trip lower. **Risk to the narrative:** it is a 60-day framework, *not* a treaty; ratification, mine-clearance and a vessel backlog mean *physical* normalisation lags the *paper* repricing, and any Israel–Lebanon flare-up reintroduces premium fast. Don't treat the price as if the barrels are already flowing.

**2) Hawkish-hold Fed regime (Warsh).** 17 Jun: rates held 3.50–3.75%, statement gutted to ~130 words, dots median to ~3.8% (a *hike*), 9/18 see ≥1 hike, Warsh declined to submit his own dot. DXY >100, 2y ~4.20%. A guidance-light Fed puts a premium on every data point. **The internal tension:** the hawkish case leans on a Mideast oil impulse that Brent is actively unwinding — a dovish walk-back becomes live if oil keeps sliding into softer US data.

Story now retires to monitoring.

**4) Thursday's US rebound vs a shut Friday.** S&P +1.08%, Nasdaq +1.91% recovered most of the FOMC drop. But with US cash shut today, the bounce can't be confirmed or faded by American flow — Europe and Asia set the tone, and any gap risk parks until Monday.

## 4. Desk Angle — flows, vol, positioning

- **Liquidity is the dominant fact today.** US fully shut means thinner books, larger slippage and headline-driven air-pockets in FX and crude — exactly where the Geneva ceremony will land. Size accordingly; this is not the day to express conviction in illiquid US-linked risk.
- **Vol:** equity vol should bleed lower mechanically over the closed session (no US gamma to hedge), but that understates *gap* risk into Monday. Oil vol is where the action is — a "sell-the-fact" on the signing vs a "physical-takes-longer" squeeze are both live.
- **Positioning skew:** (i) **gold/crypto longs** are the pressured cohort under higher front-end yields + DXY >100; further long liquidation is the pain trade. (ii) **Crude shorts** are crowded into the deal — asymmetric squeeze risk if the ceremony slips, ratification stalls, or Israel–Lebanon flares. (iii) **USD/JPY carry longs** sit right on the intervention line — convex downside if the MoF acts.
- **Rates:** front-end is doing the hawkish work (2y ~4.20%); the back end is anchored by oil disinflation → flatter curve. The asymmetry: softer US data + lower oil = a fast dovish re-think of the dots.
- **Where risk is skewed:** dollar-up/oil-down is consensus and partly spent. The contrarian tail is an oil squeeze on a Geneva hiccup feeding back into the inflation/Fed story — watch it precisely because everyone is leaning the other way.
