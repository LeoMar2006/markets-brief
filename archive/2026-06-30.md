## 3) What's Moving Markets

**1. AI complex — the scare wobbled, didn't break.** Last week's "return-on-capex" repricing (sparked by reports OpenAI may **delay its IPO to 2027** rather than price below Altman's insisted **$1tn** mark) reversed hard Monday on a South Korean AI-investment push and firm chip-demand signals. Demand leg looks intact; the bear case was always about *return* on capex, not *demand*. But the overhang is real: **SpaceX has collapsed from $225 to ~$153** since its 12 Jun debut — a live warning that public books won't automatically pay private marks — and **Anthropic** (funding at **$965bn**, now above OpenAI's **$852bn**; "Mythos 5" cleared Washington) reframes the competitive picture. Treat Monday's bounce as a relief rally inside an unresolved debate, not the all-clear.

**2. Copper's policy day.** The Section 232 refined-copper update lands today. Market sits near records on a structural ~600kt 2026 deficit (data-centres, grid, EV). A duty recommendation tightens the US physical market and widens the COMEX–LME premium; a soft/no-action update is the disappointment risk after a long build-up.

**3. US–Iran cooled — oil stayed calm throughout.** After the late-June flare-up (commercial-vessel strike, US retaliatory strikes near Hormuz), both sides agreed to halt mutual attacks ahead of **Qatar-hosted talks this week**. Brent's refusal to spike above ~$72 — through both escalation and de-escalation — underlines how well-supplied the market is. A genuine Hormuz re-closure remains the un-priced asymmetric tail; base case is it stays a watch item.

**4. Yen at the cliff edge.** USD/JPY ~161.7, a whisker from the **162** trigger. MoF has spent ~¥11.7tn YTD with little to show; the ~275bp rate gap keeps carry alive but asymmetric. The mitigant today: pressure is building into a *risk-on* tape, where intervention is less likely to coincide with a disorderly tech unwind. The tail is the overlap — a surprise MoF move into any renewed AI wobble (300–500-pip air-pockets historically).

**5. Gold back through $4,000.** The "low forming" story is on the defensive — $3,986, below the round number again, on higher-for-longer Fed, a firmer dollar and Monday's risk-on bleeding the safe-haven bid. Silver $58.80. A hot NFP Thursday would press the long further.

**6. Warsh into Sintra (tomorrow).** First overseas appearance for the new Fed chair, alongside Lagarde/Bailey/Macklem. After a hawkish-hold June (3.50–3.75%, a hike still in the 2026 dots, easing bias dropped), the market wants to know whether AI-driven financial-conditions wobble dents the hawkish lean. Low-guidance style means *any* substance moves rates. Backdrop: Supreme Court still weighing the attempt to remove a Fed governor — an independence overhang.

**7. UK politics — orderly.** Burnham's path to the Labour leadership looks like a coronation (won Makerfield by ~9.2k; no declared challenger; Streeting stood aside). **Nominations open 9 Jul, close 16 Jul; leader by ~29 Aug.** Gilts have taken the smoother transition well (10y ~4.78%); the deferred fiscal question (Burnham's borrowing/mansion-tax instincts) is a term-premium risk for later, not now. GBP firm ~1.34+.

**8. ECB — done, and quiet.** Deposit rate 2.25%, June flash PMIs soft (composite 49.5). Tomorrow's euro-area CPI (cons 3.0% headline) should reinforce "the June hike was the last." Bund 10y ~2.95%. Sintra comms are the wildcard.

- **Positioning skew is asymmetric to the hawkish side.** The pain trade is an **upside JOLTS/NFP or a hawkish Warsh** re-tightening financial conditions into thin books — that hits the most-crowded longs (gold, mega-cap AI on leverage) and would re-test the yen's 162 line from the wrong direction (USD strength, not MoF).
- **Two binary catalysts you can't hedge cheaply intraday:** the copper 232 update (headline-driven, timing uncertain) and a potential MoF yen move. Size around them.
- **Crypto is the cleanest risk-appetite tell right now** — its failure to rally with equities Monday says the bid is still convalescent. Watch BTC's $60k reclaim as confirmation (or not) of the equity rebound's durability.
