## 3. What's Moving Markets

**1) The hawkish hold — and the Warsh redesign.** The substance was the *projections*, not the rate: 2026 median to 3.8% (a hike), 9 of 18 dots above current, and Warsh pointedly **not submitting his own dot** — consistent with his long-standing scepticism of forward guidance. The statement was cut from 341 to **130 words**, all easing language removed. He also flagged **task forces to overhaul Fed operations**. Net: a Fed that's both more hawkish *and* deliberately less pre-committed — which raises the premium on every data point and every Fedspeak line from here. Risk to the hawkish read: it's a *projection*, not a decision, and the entire dot-shift rests on the Middle-East inflation impulse that oil is now actively undercutting.

**2) US–Iran MoU signed remotely; Geneva ceremony Friday.** Trump and his Iranian counterpart signed a memorandum of understanding to end the war overnight; the formal signing is Friday in Geneva, with immediate resumption of Iranian exports and a Hormuz reopening. This is what pulled Asian risk up and Brent down. **Caveat for the desk:** it remains a 60-day *framework*, not a treaty — reversibility risk is real, and physical normalisation (mine-clearance, tanker repositioning, vessel backlog) runs months behind the paper price. Expect a paper-vs-physical oil gap.

**3) UK CPI — flat headline, hot services.** Headline **2.8%** (unchanged), but **services 3.7%** (up from 3.2%) and **transport 6.8%** (highest since Dec-22, fuel/airfares/VED). On paper a hawkish services print — yet gilts *rallied* and BoE hike bets were trimmed, because the oil collapse and a softening labour market dominate the forward view. The tension the MPC must adjudicate at noon.

**4) Oil's 40% round-trip.** Brent has retraced from ~$108 (April conflict peak) to sub-$79, a 3-month low. This is the single biggest disinflation input across DM right now — it's what lets the ECB call the top, gives BoE doves cover despite hot services, and is the standing rebuttal to the Fed's hawkish dots.

**5) Asia decoupling from the Fed.** Nikkei to a record and KOSPI near one *the morning after* a hawkish FOMC is notable. BoJ at 1.0% (highest since 1995), a still-weak yen at the intervention line, the AI/semis bid, and the Iran relief are together overpowering US rate gravity. Watch whether this holds once US cash reopens.

**6) Copper grinds at records.** COMEX ~$6.40/lb with TC/RCs near zero (smelters starved of concentrate). The 30 June US copper-tariff review is the near-term catalyst keeping US vs ex-US pre-positioning alive; structurally the multi-year concentrate deficit (data-centres, grid, EV) is the story.

**7) Yen at the tripwire.** USD/JPY ~160.20 sits exactly where MoF has historically drawn the line. A hawkish-Fed dollar pushing the pair higher into a BoJ that just hited to 1.0% is the classic intervention setup — headline risk in both directions.
