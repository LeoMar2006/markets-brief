## 3. What's Moving Markets

**1. Samsung's record that nobody bought.** Prelim Q2 operating profit ~₩89.4tn (~$58.6bn), +1,811% YoY — the highest quarterly operating profit ever posted by a tech company, eclipsing Nvidia and Apple, on DRAM/HBM/NAND pricing. Yet Samsung shares fell 6%+ and dragged the Asian chip complex down (Nikkei −2.04%, SUMCO/Kioxia/Taiyo Yuden all −11%). The message: memory upcycle is real, but the stocks had already priced it, and Q3 visibility plus the AI-concentration overhang mean good news isn't enough. This is the crux of the rotation debate.

**2. US records on cyclicals, not chips.** Dow closed above 53,000 for the first time; S&P at 7,537. Monday's Nasdaq +1.12% was led by a relief bid (Western Digital +7%, Teradyne +2.8% — memory/test names benefiting from the same Samsung read that later sank Asia). The divergence between the record US indices and the semis wobble is the concentration question in real time.

**3. FOMC minutes tomorrow — the week's fulcrum.** After the shockingly soft +57k June payrolls (vs ~115k cons, prior two months revised −74k, participation down to a 2021-low 61.5%), September FedWatch has drifted to ~56% for a hike (from ~64%). Minutes will show how contested Warsh's first hawkish hold was.

**4. Yen: silent-treatment intervention risk.** USD/JPY back near 161.8–162 after Friday's payroll-driven dip to sub-161. MoF's Katayama keeps up "appropriate action at any time" language, but Tokyo has gone quiet — reportedly to catch shorts offside rather than pre-signal. A hawkish minutes read is the obvious catalyst to re-test 162+ and force Tokyo's hand.

**5. Copper: the decision that won't come.** COMEX ~$6.15/lb, holding. The Commerce report on refined-copper markets was due to the President 30 Jun; the phased universal refined duty (15% Jan-27 → 30% Jan-28) framework is drafted but **no signed proclamation has landed**. Every day of delay bleeds a little premium; a signature would snap it violently higher (Goldman flags >$14,000/t on LME if duties proceed).

**6. Oil steady-firm, disinflationary.** Brent ~$72, WTI ~$69, capped by a descending trendline. US–Iran de-escalation and normalised Hormuz shipping keep the supply story dominant; OPEC+ added a modest ~188kb/d. A benign energy backdrop reinforces the soft-labour, dovish-drift narrative.

**7. Gold consolidating.** ~$4,148, digesting the post-NFP bounce that snapped a 5-week losing streak. One good week doesn't repair June's −12.7% structural break; tomorrow's minutes are the swing factor (hawkish = risk back toward $3,950; more labour softening = upside).

**8. UK Labour leadership — nominations open in two days.** Contest opens 9 Jul, closes 16 Jul. Andy Burnham is the sole declared candidate with 200+ MP backers after Streeting stood aside; if unopposed he's leader on 17 Jul. Gilts (10y ~4.68%) and sterling ($1.335) are treating it as an orderly, priced transition — the fiscal question is deferred, not gone.

**9. Tariff-legal cliff, 24 July.** The 10% Section 122 global surcharge expires by operation of law at 12:01am ET 24 Jul (150-day statutory cap). CIT ruled it unlawful (7 May); the Federal Circuit stayed that pending appeal (11 Jun), so CBP keeps collecting. Watch for a **replacement vehicle** — reports point to a Section 301-based ~12.5% surcharge on ~46 countries being prepped. Term-premium and trade-policy tail building into the deadline.

## 4. Desk Angle — Flows, Vol, Positioning

- **Positioning is the driver, not fundamentals.** The Samsung tape is the cleanest evidence: a genuine earnings blowout that *sells off* signals crowded, over-owned semis being de-risked. Momentum/CTA and vol-target books that rode the Q2 doubling of the SOX are the marginal sellers into any AI wobble; the pain trade is a continued grind higher in the Dow/value that leaves fast-money underweight cyclicals chasing.
- **The US-vs-Asia whipsaw is a gift to two-way vol, not a directional signal.** Don't over-read Monday's Nasdaq bounce as an all-clear — Asia said otherwise 12 hours later. Expect elevated intraday chop in semis/AI-adjacent names into next week's bank earnings and hyperscaler capex guides.
- **Rates: coiled spring into the minutes.** 2y pinned ~4.13%, 10y ~4.49%. Risk is asymmetric around tomorrow — a hawkish minutes read (given the soft-NFP dovish repricing) has more room to move the front end and the dollar than a dovish one. Watch USD/JPY 162 as the intervention line-in-the-sand.
- **Dollar smile risk.** DXY at a 3-week low but stabilising. If the minutes lean hawkish *and* risk wobbles on the AI question, the dollar can catch a bid from both sides — squeezing the crowded post-payrolls short-USD.
- **Skew:** copper carries the fattest binary — a signed refined-duty proclamation is a violent-upside gap risk that spot doesn't price. Gold's risk is two-sided but skewed lower into a hawkish minutes.
