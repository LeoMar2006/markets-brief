## 3. What's Moving Markets

**1. Payrolls set the Fed's next move.** With the Fed on a hawkish hold (3.50–3.75%, a 2026 hike still in the dots) and JOLTS at a 2-year high, a hot print reinforces "no cuts, maybe a hike" and extends dollar strength — pressuring the yen toward MoF's hand. A soft print (ADP already flagged easing) would be the first crack in the higher-for-longer thesis and could take the dollar off its highs. Wage growth is the swing factor.

**2. Dollar at 15-month high; yen at 40-year low.** DXY ~101.4 on Warsh's hawkishness plus soft euro CPI and fiscally-dovish Japan. USD/JPY pushed to ~162.8 intraday (strongest since 1986), through the ~162 line the MoF has treated as a trigger. Tokyo has spent ~¥11.7tn YTD with little to show; the ~250–275bp rate gap keeps the carry alive. Unilateral intervention likely only buys time — coordinated US-Japan action would bite, but there's no sign of it.

**3. Warsh's Sintra debut — hawkish.** In his first major international appearance as Fed Chair, Warsh said inflation "remains too elevated" and prices "are too high," even as officials grow more open to AI's disinflationary potential. Low-information style as expected, but the tone-parsing landed hawkish and underwrote the dollar bid.

**4. Euro-area inflation cools to 2.8%.** Flash June HICP fell from 3.2% to **2.8%** (below 3.0% cons), services 3.2% (from 3.5%), energy base effects fading. This tempers the market's flirtation with further ECB hikes and keeps EUR/USD pinned near a 1-year low ~1.14 — euro weakness as much as dollar strength.

**5. Copper — refined 232 decision still unsigned.** The Commerce (Lutnick) domestic-market report to the President was due 30 Jun; the framework is a phased universal refined-copper duty (15% Jan-27 → 30% Jan-28). No signed proclamation has surfaced. COMEX fell 1.4% to **$6.10/lb** — the built-up inventory air-pocket the desk warned about if the decision slips or softens. Goldman still flags >$14,000/t if duties proceed.

**6. Gold's long stays broken.** After −12.7% in June (worst month since 2008), gold hovers just below **$4,000**, an ~8-month low, squeezed by higher-for-longer and a firm dollar. A hot NFP is the clean catalyst to press it lower; a soft print is the near-term relief valve. Base to watch: $3,950–4,000.

**7. Crypto still won't confirm.** BTC ~$60k, ETH ~$1,580 — did not join the equity record. The cleanest tell that the melt-up is narrow and tech-led, not broad risk-on. A BTC reclaim of $60k+ would be the confirmation signal; absent it, treat equity strength as concentrated.

**8. Tariff-legal overhang building for late July.** Section 122's 10% across-the-board surcharge expires **24 Jul**; the CIT ruled against it in May (under appeal, tariffs still collected) after SCOTUS struck the IEEPA "Liberation Day" tariffs in February. Not today's story, but a term-premium and trade-policy tail worth pre-positioning for as the deadline nears.

## 4. Desk Angle — Flows, Vol, Positioning

- **Positioning is the risk, not the narrative.** Equity longs are crowded and *narrow* (chips/AI carried Q2; crypto and breadth are not confirming). That's a tape vulnerable to an air-pocket on any AI-monetisation scare or a hot-wage payroll that lifts real yields — the pullback in semis yesterday is the tell to respect.
- **FX is where the convexity is.** Long-USD/short-JPY is consensus and profitable but asymmetric: you're collecting carry in front of a central bank with ¥-buying intent and a live intervention trigger, into thin Friday liquidity. Historic MoF moves have produced 300–500-pip air-pockets. Size for the gap, not the drift.
- **Rates:** front end anchored by the hawkish hold; the 2s10s and 10y are the payroll expression. A soft NFP steepens via the front end and caps the dollar; a hot one flattens and extends USD.
- **Vol:** expect an event-vol bid into 13:30, then a fast unwind. Skew favours owning cheap JPY convexity (intervention tail) and modest downside protection on the narrow-long semis complex over the holiday gap.
- **Commodities skew:** copper's disappointment air-pocket has room lower near-term if no signature comes; gold's pain trade is still lower on a hot print. Brent's disinflationary drift cuts *against* the hawkish-Fed story — a quiet offset few are weighting.
