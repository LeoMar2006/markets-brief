## 5. Desk Angle — flows, vol, positioning

- **The set-up is a positioning washout, not a fundamental one.** TSMC just *upgraded* the demand curve while the highest-beta AI longs (memory) get liquidated. That divergence is where the opportunity — and the trap — sits: European equipment names (ASML/ASMI/BESI) have a fundamentally better hand than the tape implies, but they carry the semis-complex beta and can be dragged regardless in the flush. Sizing and defined-risk matter more than direction here.
- **Skew:** risk is skewed to a **decoupling snap-back** in EU front-end/packaging equipment once the memory unwind exhausts — but timing is a flows call, and a third down-day in Asia says it isn't done. Fade panic in *quality capex-beneficiaries*, not in memory beta.
- **Rotation flows** into staples/banks are real and have a catalyst today (Nordic banks). This is the lower-variance way to be long Europe into the semis noise.
- **Rates/vol:** Bund at cycle-high 3.1% on oil pass-through caps rate-sensitives; any HICP services surprise at 10:00 is the swing factor for European duration and, by extension, real-estate/utilities.
- **Index mechanics:** with STOXX bellwethers printing in a 06:00–10:30 burst, expect the open to gap rather than drift — liquidity-provision, not trend, early doors.
