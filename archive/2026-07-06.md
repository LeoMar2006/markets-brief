## 3. What's Moving Markets

**1) Soft June payrolls — the first real crack.** +57k vs ~+115k consensus, with Apr/May revised down ~74k. The unemployment rate "fell" to 4.2% only because participation dropped 0.3pp to 61.5% (a 2021 low) — a low-quality decline, not strength. Leisure/hospitality shed 61k. This kills the near-term *hike* case without forcing a *cut*, which is exactly the "soft landing / dovish drift" cocktail risk assets like. **The caveat the desk should hold:** one print after a run of firm data is a data-point, not a trend — and Wednesday's ISM Prices or a hot CPI could re-arm the hawks.

**2) The AI/semis unwind — rotation, not reversal (yet).** SOX −6.7% on the week while the Dow made new highs is a textbook rotation out of the most crowded, most rate-sensitive part of the tape into value/cyclicals. Whether this is healthy broadening or the first leg of a concentration unwind is the single most important tape question for H2. Monday's Nikkei/chip rebound argues "just a wobble" — but hyperscaler capex guides in the mid-July earnings season are the real test.

**3) FOMC minutes Wednesday — Warsh's first fingerprints.** With no dot from the Chair himself and a new task force reviewing forward guidance, the minutes are the market's first proper look at how the Warsh Fed thinks. A hawkish tone (defending the 2026-hike dot) collides head-on with the soft-NFP dovish repricing; a softer tone accelerates it.

**4) Dollar rolls over.** DXY down ~0.7% on the week off a one-year-plus high. The whole G10 move — EUR/USD off its low, yen off its 40-year low, cable steady — is the mirror image of US front-end rates coming in. If the minutes read hawkish, expect a fast dollar retrace and the yen back toward 162.

**5) Gold and silver find a floor.** After gold's −12.7% June (worst month since Oct-2008), the metals snapped back on the dovish data — gold's first weekly gain in ~five weeks, silver back to ~$62.8. The pain trade is no longer one-way, but one bounce doesn't repair a broken structure; hawkish minutes could re-break gold toward $3,950.

**6) Copper's air-pocket persists.** Commerce's copper-market report to the President was due 30 Jun; there is **still no signed refined-metal proclamation**. The market had positioned for a phased universal refined duty (15% Jan-27 → 30% Jan-28); the delay is the disappointment the desk flagged, with COMEX at ~$6.10/lb, a ~$400/t COMEX–LME spread and record ~650kt inventories. A signed proclamation is a violent upside snap (Goldman >$14,000/t); continued silence bleeds the premium.

**7) Yen — intervention risk downgraded, not gone.** USD/JPY sits ~161 after tagging 162.83 (a four-decade low) on 1 Jul. Soft NFP relieved the pressure without the MoF having to act; FinMin Katayama keeps up the verbal warnings and there are reports Japan may stop pre-signalling operations to catch shorts offside. ~¥11.7tn spent YTD; a ~250bp rate gap keeps the carry alive. Renewed dollar strength re-tests 162 and the intervention question.

**8) Oil stays disinflationary.** Brent ~$71–72 near pre-conflict lows as Hormuz shipping normalises and US–Iran talks grind on. OPEC+'s modest ~188kb/d add signals confidence in stability. This is a quiet tailwind to the soft-inflation narrative — with Hormuz re-closure the un-priced asymmetric tail.

## 4. Desk Angle — Flows, Vol, Positioning

- **Positioning is the risk.** The pain in semis last week is what happens when the most crowded, most-owned complex meets a rate-sensitive wobble. Concentration in mega-cap tech is precisely what a rate repricing punishes first — and it's a two-way risk now: dovish minutes could re-inflate the melt-up just as easily as hawkish minutes crush the rotation losers.
- **Skew:** Into Wednesday, the market is leaning dovish (dollar down, gold/crypto up, front-end rallied). That makes the *asymmetric surprise* a hawkish minutes read — thin summer liquidity would amplify a snap-back in the dollar, USD/JPY and gold. Cheap convexity is in owning a hawkish tail.
- **Liquidity:** First full session back from a US long weekend into a light data day — expect thin books until the 15:00 ISM, then real two-way risk into Wednesday. Gap risk on the Monday equity reopen after Asia's strong lead.
- **Vol:** Rates vol should stay bid into the minutes; equity vol was under-pricing the semis unwind and may stay jumpy until the mid-July hyperscaler guides clear.
- **Cross-asset tell:** Watch whether **BTC holds $60k** and whether **chips reclaim leadership**. Both holding = genuine risk-on broadening; either failing = the rotation was a positioning unwind, not a regime change.
