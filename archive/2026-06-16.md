## 3. What's Moving Markets

**1. US–Iran deal — the dominant macro driver.** Framework agreement reached 14 Jun; US naval blockade lifted, Strait of Hormuz (~20% of world oil/LNG) to reopen "toll-free." **Signing set for Friday 19 Jun in Switzerland**; preparatory meetings with each side in Doha this week, then technical talks. The ceasefire is a **60-day** extension, not a final treaty — reversibility risk is real. Physical normalisation (mine-clearance, vessel backlog) runs "many months" per US officials, so the paper-vs-physical oil gap persists even as Brent prices the all-clear. **This is a framework/MOU, not a signed peace — fade complacency around the Friday signing actually executing and around Iran's parliamentary/term disputes.**

**2. BoJ hikes to 1.00% — but the messenger undercuts the message.** First 1% policy rate in 31 years, on broadening inflation (May corporate goods prices +6.3% y/y, energy-driven). The hike was ~96% priced, so the catalyst was always *guidance tone* — and with **Ueda hospitalised and Uchida deputising**, the bank avoided an aggressive forward signal. Net: JPY stayed soft (~160), carry trades survive for now. Watch Ueda's health/return and any clarifying communication; an unexpectedly hawkish follow-through is the tail that unwinds global carry.

**3. Warsh's Fed — the week's binary.** Consensus: explicit shift from easing bias to **neutral**, first cut pushed out. Genuine structural wildcard: Warsh may **abolish/redesign the dot plot and forward guidance** as soon as this meeting. Hawkish dots (or a guidance vacuum read hawkishly) tightens conditions without a hike — hits 2y, gold, the long end. Note one tension in the data: stickier US inflation prints (CPI ~3.8% cited in some FX commentary) argue against any dovish tilt.

**4. Oil at two-month lows.** Brent sub-$83, WTI ~$80. The risk premium from the Feb-28 war is draining as Hormuz reopens. **Risk to the bearish narrative:** reopening is logistical and slow; any signing hiccup or Israel-Lebanon flare-up snaps the premium back fast. The physical barrel isn't flowing yet.

**5. Gold's stubborn bid.** ~$4,300, up a third straight session *into* a risk-on, de-escalation tape — counter-intuitive and telling. Structural support (EM/central-bank buying, soft DXY, capped real rates) is overriding the geopolitical-premium unwind. Wednesday's dots are the near-term binary for a crowded long.

**6. Copper firm on deficit + EM optics.** LME ~$6.4–6.5/lb. Mining-study base-case copper assumptions have climbed ~60% since 2020 (~$4.80/lb avg early-2026), and Chinese smelters are scrambling for feedstock (pyrite buying). Structural deficit story (AI data-centres, grid, EV) intact; deal-driven EM-growth optics an incremental tailwind.

**7. ZEW snaps back.** German expectations leapt to 47.5 — the cleanest early read that the Iran de-escalation is repairing European sentiment. Supports the EUR-higher, EZ-reflation impulse, but cuts against any lingering ECB dovish pivot.

**8. SpaceX (SPCX) week two.** Post-IPO monitoring — listed 12 Jun at $135, debut close $161 (+19%), market cap >$2tn. $75bn capital absorption may cap marginal risk appetite; S&P-inclusion profitability hurdle not yet met.

- **JPY/carry:** the dovish-messenger outcome keeps carry alive, but USD/JPY at ~160 sits on the **MoF intervention tripwire** with a *just-hiked* central bank. Asymmetric: limited topside (intervention + rate differential compression), fat left tail if Ueda returns hawkish or risk wobbles → global carry/EMFX unwind.
- **Rates:** front-end fully priced for a hold; the risk is a **hawkish guidance shift** (or guidance abolition read hawkishly) steepening via the back end or bear-flattening the 2s. 10y at 4.42% has rallied on the oil/breakeven move — vulnerable to a dot-plot disappointment.
- **Gold:** crowded long into a binary. Structural bid is real but the pain trade Wednesday is a hawkish-dots flush. Size accordingly.
- **Equities skew:** upside is deal-confirmation + dovish Fed; downside is signing slips OR Warsh out-hawks a market that's already chased. Risk-reward for fresh longs at these levels is poor pre-FOMC.
