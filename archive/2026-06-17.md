## 3) What's Moving Markets

**1. Warsh's first FOMC — the dots are the event.** Hold is a near-certainty; the story is whether the SEP shifts the bias from "easing" to "neutral" — or further. With CPI at **4.2%** and rising, the tail is a 2026 *hike* dot appearing, or hawkish-by-omission if Warsh strips back the dot plot/forward guidance (long-flagged as his reform agenda). A guidance vacuum from a hawk-leaning new chair tightens conditions on its own. Dovish surprise looks low-odds against a 4.2% print.

**2. US–Iran deal → oil round-trip.** Framework reached 14 Jun; US lifted the naval blockade, Hormuz (~20% of seaborne oil/LNG) to reopen "toll-free." **Electronic signing Fri 19 Jun, Switzerland**, then 60 days of nuclear talks. Brent has round-tripped from $108 (April) to **~$81**. Caveat for the desk: this is a **60-day ceasefire framework, not a final treaty** — reversibility risk is real (Israel–Lebanon flare-ups have already threatened it once), and physical normalisation (mine-clearance, vessel backlog) runs *months*, so paper prices have moved faster than barrels will.

**3. Sticky US inflation vs. the disinflation trade.** The market is trading the *forward* oil-led disinflation while the *spot* print is 4.2% and climbing. That gap is the single biggest two-way risk in the room today — bonds have rallied on the former; the Fed has to respond to the latter.

**4. UK CPI → BoE.** May CPI (07:00) seen ticking to **~3.0%** from 2.8%, services sticky ~3.9%. BoE expected to **hold 3.75%** Thursday (8–1 hold in April, the dissent a *hawk*). Pill and Greene lean toward a hike; oil disinflation is the doves' offset. A hot CPI hands the hawks ammunition and is GBP-supportive.

**5. BoJ at 1.00%, Nikkei at records.** BoJ hiked to **1.00%** (highest since 1995) on the 16th, ~fully priced; bond-taper pause until after Apr-2027. Nikkei printing **>70,000** on AI leadership (~+30% YTD). Twin risks: stretched valuations and a USD/JPY pinned near 160 — the carry trade is alive but on a short leash into any intervention or hawkish BoJ follow-through.

**6. Gold's stubborn bid.** Holding ~**$4,351** through a risk-on, de-escalation tape — structural EM/central-bank buying, soft DXY and capped real rates overriding the geopolitical-premium unwind. Citi's $4,500 target underlines the conviction. But it's a crowded long and **tonight's dots are the near-term binary**: a hawkish SEP is the cleanest catalyst to shake it.

**7. Copper's quiet squeeze.** LME ~**$13,000/t**, just off January's $13,300 record; benchmark TC/RCs near **zero** = smelters starved of concentrate. Refined deficit ~**330kt** for 2026, structural concentrate shortfall for years (AI data-centres, grid, EV). EM-growth optics from the deal are an incremental tailwind to an already-tight book.

**8. Dow record vs. tech wobble.** Tuesday's tape — Dow record, Nasdaq -1.15% — is a cleaner rotation signal than the index headline: lower oil + lower yields favour cyclicals/value/small-cap *breadth*, while crowded mega-cap tech de-risks into event risk. Whether that rotation has legs hinges on the dots.

## 4) Desk Angle — flows, vol, positioning

- **Event vol is concentrated on 19:00–19:30.** With a hold ~fully priced, realised won't come from the *rate* — it comes from the **dot distribution and Warsh's framing**. Front-end (2y, SOFR) and gold are the cleanest expressions of a hawkish-dots surprise; index vol is the messier one given the rotation already underway.
- **Positioning is asymmetric on the doves' side.** Rates have rallied on forward disinflation while spot CPI is 4.2%. If the SEP leans hawkish (or guidance is hollowed out), the pain trade is a back-up in the front end, a stronger dollar, and a gold/duration shake — i.e., the consensus "deal = disinflation = lower-for-longer" book is the crowded one.
- **Oil: paper has outrun physical.** Length has chased Brent down to $81 on a framework that isn't signed and barrels that won't flow for months. Skew favours owning cheap topside/optionality against deal-reversibility (signing slips, Israel–Lebanon, Iranian domestic ratification) rather than pressing fresh shorts at three-month lows.
- **JPY carry on a tripwire.** USD/JPY ~160 with the BoJ now at 1.00% and MoF watching — fine while vol is low, but it's the classic fast-unwind candidate if the Fed jolts global front-ends or risk wobbles.
- **Equity internals > headline.** Watch breadth/Russell and the cyclical-vs-tech spread for the real read on whether the rotation is a trend or a one-day de-risk.
