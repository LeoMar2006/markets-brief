## 5. Desk Angle — flows, vol, positioning

- **Vol:** Expect European index vol to firm on the geopolitical re-pricing after a compressed early-July. The asymmetry is toward **more** vol: a Hormuz headline gaps oil and cyclicals; a de-escalation headline gaps them back. Own optionality rather than delta here.
- **Positioning skew:** Two crowded longs are the pressure points — **defense** (momentum, now unwinding) and **long-duration quality/luxury** (rates-sensitive). Both can keep bleeding independent of fundamentals. The **energy** underweight that most European funds carry is the pain trade if Brent holds >$80.
- **Rates leg matters as much as oil:** the hawkish minutes cap the "buy-the-dip on rate-cut hope" reflex that worked post-NFP. With Sept-hike odds ~70%, dips in rate-sensitive Europe are less obviously bought.
- **Flow read:** FTSE 100's relative outperformance (energy weight + weak GBP) is the classic risk-off-into-oil rotation within Europe — expect UK large-cap energy/defensive relative bid to persist while the conflict headline is live.
- **Risk skew:** Net **negative gamma feel** into a headline-driven tape. Keep gross tight; the cleanest expressions are **long energy / short airlines** and **long oil vol / long index puts** rather than outright directional index shorts into a possibly-fast de-escalation.

## 7. European Equity Pitches

**1) TotalEnergies (TTE FP) — LONG (oil-beta on the Hormuz premium).**
Thesis: cleanest liquid European play on a re-pricing Brent (>$79 and rising) with a >5% dividend and buyback floor limiting downside if oil fades. Integrated model dampens the pure-E&P beta while still capturing the shock. Instrument: cash long, or a **Aug 690/... call spread** (calendar-appropriate strikes) to define risk into the print. Catalyst: **Q2/H1 results late July (2025 print: 24 Jul)** into a live oil-supply story. Risk: rapid Iran de-escalation collapses the crude premium; refining margins soft.

**2) Rheinmetall (RHM GY) — LONG the dip (contrarian).**
Thesis: -3.5% sector day was crowded-long profit-taking on a risk-off tape, **not** a fundamental hit — the European re-armament order book (Ukraine/NATO 3%+ GDP spend pledges) is unrelated to Hormuz, and a hot Middle East arguably reinforces the defense-spend narrative. Instrument: cash long on weakness, or **defined-risk call spread** to cap the valuation risk. Catalyst: **H1 results ~early Aug (2025: 7 Aug)** + rolling order-intake headlines. Risk: momentum unwind extends; the stock is richly valued and a further broad de-risking overrides the story.

**3) easyJet (EZJ LN) — LONG (event/merger situation).**
Thesis: board "minded to recommend" Castlelake's 690p cash (~75% premium); a binding offer converts most of the residual spread. Any oil-driven wobble in the shares is an entry into a largely-agreed deal. Instrument: cash long into completion (tight, defined event risk); size for a break scenario. Catalyst: **PUSU deadline 3 Aug** — binding offer or walk-away. Risk: **EU/UK airline foreign-ownership rules** block a US private-credit owner; regulatory delay; broad oil-shock sentiment weighs on the standalone downside.

**4) Intesa Sanpaolo (ISP IM) — LONG (rates + sell-side tailwind).**

A risk-off tape that spares the FTSE-style value/energy complex tends to spare well-capitalised banks too. Instrument: cash long, or a European banks pair (long ISP / short a rate-insensitive defensive). Catalyst: **Q2 results late July**; ECB/BTP-spread stability. Risk: a growth scare from sustained high oil + hawkish Fed hits credit/loan-growth; BTP-Bund spread widening on Italian fiscal noise.
