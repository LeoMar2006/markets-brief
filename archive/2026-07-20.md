## 3. What's Moving Markets (Europe-first)

1. **Hormuz tail fires — the ceasefire is off.** The mid-July truce collapsed over the weekend: multiple new US strike waves (reports of 80+ targets across the campaign), Iranian retaliation on Gulf states, a naval blockade near Iranian ports, and **Hormuz transits down >50% w/w**. Brent +~14% on the week to ~$88–90. **The market is pricing serious disruption but *not yet* a fully enforced, sustained ~20%-of-seaborne closure** — that remains the un-priced tail to the upside for oil (and the downside for risk). Two-sided: these episodes have whipsawed (Trump dropped a mooted Hormuz transit fee on 14 Jul before re-escalation), so headline risk cuts both ways intraday.

2. **The chip/AI flush is still running — and it's positioning, not (yet) demand.** TSMC's 16 Jul **beat-and-raise** (Q2 rev $40.2bn +34%, FY26 rev growth guided >40%, capex lifted to ~$60bn+) *upgraded* the AI-capex curve, yet the ADR fell ~5% on the news and the complex kept selling: **SMH ETF ~−9% on the week / ~−17% MTD.** ASML's own Q2 was strong (rev €9.33bn, FY guide raised to €43–45bn) and it *still* fell −3.8%. Read: the highest-beta, most-crowded AI longs (memory: SK Hynix, Samsung) are unwinding, dragging fundamentally-de-risked European equipment names with them. The Moonshot open-model headline added a "value-capture" scare. **Watch BESI (Thu 23) and ASMI (28 Jul) for the capex read-through — the first real test of whether EU equipment decouples.**

3. **Defensive rotation is the live European trade.** Utilities, telecoms and energy caught the outflow from tech/banks on Friday. With oil bid and a risk-off macro, that leadership can persist; the FTSE 100's energy/defensive tilt is why it was the only major to close green.

4. **Gold is *not* working as the war hedge — rates are winning.** Spot slid below $4,000 to ~$3,992 (biggest weekly loss since early June) even as the Gulf escalated. The hawkish-Fed/real-yield regime is overpowering the geopolitical bid — the single most important cross-asset tell on the board right now. A decisive break >$4,100 would signal the geopolitics is finally re-rating; until then, "rates > war."

5. **Telecom Italia / Poste — deal live from today.** TIM's board on **Sat 18 Jul unanimously deemed Poste Italiane's voluntary tender/exchange offer fair** (terms €1.67 cash + 0.218 new Poste shares per TIM share; headline value cited ~€10.8bn, figures vary across outlets). **Acceptance window 20 Jul–11 Sep** (Consob-approved); Poste already ~20%. A rare piece of European large-cap M&A completing the domestic-champion logic.

6. **Sell-side turns *more* bullish on Europe despite the tape.** On 17 Jul, **UBS, BofA, Deutsche Bank and Kepler Cheuvreux all raised STOXX 600 year-end targets** (UBS the most bullish, ~+8% upside). The strategist community is treating the chip flush + war as a positioning/valuation event, not a fundamental European downgrade — worth weighing against the risk-off price action.

7. **UK political handover — orderly so far.** Burnham's commitment to the existing fiscal rules and a market-friendly Chancellor pick has kept gilts/GBP calm (sterling one-year high). The deferred fiscal question is a later-term term-premium risk, not a today risk — but the Cabinet/Chancellor confirmation today is the near-term catalyst.

8. **Miners: gold's grip loosens.** mining.com: the top-50 miners shed ~$228bn of market cap in Q2 as gold rolled over; **BHP +16% (~$28bn)**, **Rio Tinto** reclaimed #2 and raised guidance 14 Jul on high gold prices + productivity; copper the relative bright spot on supply stress (Chile atmospheric-river risk, Mideast acid squeeze). Basic-resources beta is a lever on both the China reflation tape (HK/Shanghai up) and the commodity supply story.

## 5. Desk Angle — flows, vol, positioning

- **Where risk is skewed:** Long-energy/short-duration-tech is the crowded expression of both the war and the chip flush — meaning a **sudden de-escalation headline is the pain trade** (oil gaps down, semis squeeze up). Given how these Iran episodes have whipsawed, sizing into strength on either leg is the risk. The un-priced *upside* for oil is a genuinely enforced, sustained Hormuz closure; the un-priced *downside* is a ceasefire re-establishment.
- **European vol:** expect the VSTOXX bid to persist while the chip complex and the Gulf are both live; the index-level moves understate single-name dispersion (tech −2.3% vs utilities +1.3% is a >3.5-point sector spread). This is a **dispersion/rotation tape**, not a beta-down tape — favour relative-value over outright direction.
- **Banks:** the "record US season → EU read-through" is real but the sell-side already flags EU banks decelerating; SX7P into DB (29 Jul)/Santander (22 Jul)/BNP (23 Jul) is a *show-me* on NII and payouts, not a gimme. Friday's −1.3% suggests the read-through is not being front-run.
- **Semis:** BESI (23 Jul) and ASMI (28 Jul) are the near-term catalysts to test the "EU equipment decouples on fundamentals" thesis. A strong capex-levered print into a washed-out tape is the asymmetric setup; a miss confirms the de-rating.
- **Oil-levered equities:** European majors (Shell, BP, TotalEnergies, Equinor) and oil services are the direct beneficiaries; tanker names (see Aurelion's "trade of the year" tanker thesis) are the second-derivative Hormuz play as rates spike on rerouting.
