## 3. What's Moving Markets

**1) Micron's blowout re-rates the whole memory complex.** FQ3: EPS $25.11 vs ~$20.3 est; revenue $41.46bn vs ~$35.3bn; gross margin a record 84.9%; data-centre revenue >$25bn in the quarter (>$100bn annualised run-rate). Management flagged **17 Strategic Customer Agreements** locking in guaranteed compute and revenue, HBM sold out through 2026 into early 2027, and HBM4 ramping ~2x the pace of HBM3. This is the single most important data point of the week: it directly refutes Tuesday's "AI-memory bubble" panic with hard numbers and forward visibility. The risk to the bull case is no longer demand — it's **how much of this is already in price** after a parabolic YTD, and whether 2027 pricing (not 2026, which is booked) can sustain these margins.

**3) Gold breaks $4,000.** Spot ~$4,016, intraday below $4k, lowest since November 2025, −3% Wed and **−11.7% over four weeks**. The crowded haven long is being force-fed into a wall of strong dollar + firm real yields + risk-on equities. **6) Oil stays soft, doing the disinflation work.** Brent ~$73, WTI ~$71 — low $70s, ~3-month lows — as the Iran de-escalation holds (Hormuz open, 60-day license to 21 Aug). This is the wildcard for the Fed's hawkish dots, which were partly built on an oil impulse that has since collapsed.

## 4. Desk Angle

**Positioning is the whole story.** Tuesday's −10% KOSPI day was a crowded-long flush in the single most consensus trade on the planet (AI memory / HBM). Micron just handed the longs a fundamental reason to re-engage, and Asia did so violently. The question for the London open is **whether the re-grossing is durable or a relief bounce into supply.** Two tells to watch today: (i) does the move hold through the US cash open and the PCE print, or fade like the 24 Jun US session did; (ii) Nasdaq breadth — is this megacap/semis-only, or does it broaden.

**Vol/skew:** expect realised vol to stay elevated both ways — this is a market that did −10% and +5% on consecutive sessions in the same names. Gamma is short and reflexive around the memory complex. Into a 13:30 PCE that could break either way, the risk-reward favours owning optionality over directional delta in the front end and in USD pairs.

**Where risk is skewed:**
- **Long USD / short duration** remains the path of least resistance until PCE proves otherwise. A *soft* PCE is the higher-pain surprise — it's the one outcome positioning isn't set for, and it would snap the dollar/gold/real-yield complex hard.
- **The AI-memory long is re-crowding into strength.** Great fundamentals, but you're buying after a +5%/+11% Asia session and a parabolic YTD. The pain trade from here is a *second* positioning flush, not a fundamental disappointment.
- **USD/JPY ~161.5** is the cleanest asymmetric short-gamma risk on the board: carry pays you to be long until the MoF doesn't let it. Don't be the last one long dollar-yen into a Tokyo fix.
- **Gold sub-$4,000** — dip-buyers (the structural CB/EM bid) have a line in the sand somewhere here; a hawkish PCE could overrun it, a soft one could mark the low.
