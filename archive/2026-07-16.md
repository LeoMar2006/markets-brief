## 3. What's Moving Markets (Europe-first)

1. **The AI-capex trade is being stress-tested — and TSMC decides the next leg.** ASML *raised* full-year guidance for the second time (to €43–45bn) on surging AI demand, yet the stock faded from +7% to flat and Asian memory names were crushed overnight (SK Hynix −11%). This is the classic late-cycle tell: fundamentals still improving, but the *stock reaction* deteriorating as positioning gets crowded. TSMC's guide today is the arbiter — a raise + strong CoWoS/2nm commentary stabilises European semis (ASML, ASM International, BESI, Infineon); a hedged tone extends the flush. Trade the tone, not the headline number.

2. **Richemont's blowout re-rated European luxury.** Q1 sales **+20% at constant currency to €6.3bn** (vs ~€5.9bn expected), with **Jewellery Maisons (Cartier, Van Cleef) +24% cc** and even the long-weak Specialist Watchmakers +8% cc, improving sequentially. Americas +27%, Asia-Pac +21%, Japan +36%. The stock jumped **~6.7%** and dragged the whole luxury complex **+3.2%** (LVMH, Kering, Swatch, Burberry) — the strongest signal in over a year that the aspirational-luxury demand pothole is filling in. Sets a high bar into LVMH/Kering's own updates.

3. **Record US-financials season = a loud read-through to European asset gatherers and IBs.** With **BlackRock (+5%)** capping the run — record ETF/iShares flows, EPS $13.91 — and GS/JPM/MS all beating, the read-through fans out: **asset managers Amundi, DWS, Schroders, abrdn** (flow-and-fee-levered) and **markets-IBs Deutsche Bank, Barclays, UBS, BNP, SocGen**, whose Q2s land over the next fortnight (DB ~23–24 Jul). HSBC has already moved eurozone equities — banks specifically — to overweight.

4. **PPI extended the disinflation relief, but Warsh keeps leaning against it.** A soft June PPI on top of the CPI shock cements July-hike-off and pulls US yields lower — supportive for duration-sensitive European sectors. But Warsh used two days of testimony to stress the Fed isn't done and rates remain "a tool," keeping ~50% priced for September and a lid on how far the dovish read can run. Today's retail sales is the swing data.

5. **Oil is fading the tail even with the blockade live.** Brent slipped to ~$84.7 as the market prices *disruption, not enforced closure* — the Hormuz naval blockade is in force but crude keeps transiting, and Trump abandoned the 20% transit toll. European oil & gas gave back some of the week's outperformance. The un-priced tail (a genuinely enforced ~20%-of-seaborne shutdown) remains, but the premium is bleeding.

6. **easyJet bidding war still live into August.** Apollo's **715p (~£5.7bn)** tops Castlelake's 690p; the board is "no longer minded to recommend" Castlelake and minded to recommend Apollo — but both remain *possible* offers, not firm. **PUSU: Castlelake 3 Aug, Apollo 7 Aug.** Stock below 715p prices unresolved contest/foreign-ownership-approval risk.

7. **M&A pulse elsewhere:** **Akzo Nobel** received a **€7.5bn Nippon Paint offer for its Dulux/decorative-paints arm** — a chunky portfolio catalyst; **mining M&A** rolls on with Anglo American/Teck (~$53bn, copper-focused) reportedly nearing completion even as the basic-resources sector lagged yesterday.

8. **Gold's non-reaction remains the quiet macro tell.** ~$4,017, capped near $4,000 through a live Hormuz blockade — real yields and a firm-ish (if softening) dollar keep beating the war bid. A break >$4,100 would be the genuine geopolitical re-rate; it still hasn't come.

## 5. Desk Angle — flows, vol, positioning

- **The pain trade flipped inside 48 hours.** Tuesday it was *up* (soft CPI squeezed shorts, KOSPI +7%); by Thursday it is *chips-down* (KOSPI −6.3%, ASML fade) even on a *guidance raise*. That whip tells you semis positioning is crowded and jumpy — the reaction function has decoupled from the fundamentals. Into TSMC, respect two-way gap risk in ASML/ASM/BESI/Infineon; a strong-guide relief bounce is as likely as a continuation flush.
- **The cleaner conviction is in luxury and financials, not tech.** Richemont's beat is a genuine demand inflection (jewellery +24% cc, Japan +36%) with sector-wide read-through, and the US-financials clean sweep gives asset gatherers and markets-IBs a fundamentals-backed bid. These are *earnings-driven* longs, not positioning trades — a better risk/reward than chasing a semis bounce.
- **Banks: selective, not blanket.** The markets/IB blowout is most relevant to **DB, Barclays, UBS, BNP, SocGen** and the flow-levered **asset managers** (Amundi, DWS). Pure-domestic/retail banks share less. HSBC's OW upgrade adds a top-down tailwind.
- **Vol:** US VIX contained (~16) even through the chip rout — the weakness is rotational, not systemic. That keeps *owning* optionality around today's binaries (TSMC, retail sales) relatively cheap; European V2X similarly subdued. If you want semis exposure, defined-risk (call/put spreads) beats naked into TSMC.
- **Where risk is skewed:** constructive on the non-semi tape (records on Wall Street, luxury inflection, disinflation), but with two identifiable landmines — (i) **TSMC guiding cautiously** and dragging the whole AI-capex complex including EU semis, and (ii) a **hot US retail sales** reviving the hawkish-Warsh September-hike narrative and lifting yields. Both are today.
