## 3. What's Moving Markets

**1. AI-memory unwind / Micron as the swing factor.** The two-day drawdown in semis is the market's dominant force. Valuation anxiety crystallised in Korea (circuit breakers) and in US memory/AI mega-caps. The bull case — HBM sold out through 2026, HBM4 ramping ahead of schedule — is intact on fundamentals; the bear case is that *positioning and price* ran far ahead of even a strong fundamental story. Micron tonight adjudicates.

**2. SK Hynix overtakes Samsung as Korea's most valuable company.** On an HBM-fuelled +340% YTD run, SK Hynix ($1.35tn mkt cap) edged past Samsung for the first time since 2000 — a marker of how concentrated the AI-memory bid has become (SK Hynix ~61% of global HBM). Concentration cuts both ways: it's why the unwind was so violent.

**3. Hawkish-hold Fed vs deflating oil.** The June FOMC (hold at 3.50–3.75%, dots flipped to a 2026 *hike*, 9/18 now pencil ≥1 hike) was built partly on an oil/inflation impulse that has since collapsed with Brent into the mid-$70s. 2y at ~4.20% and DXY at a 1-yr high reflect the hawkish dots; the tension is whether softer data forces a dovish re-think. PCE Thursday is the next test.

**4. Iran de-escalation = the disinflation valve.** Brent ~$76–77 / WTI ~$73 sit near 3-month lows after the Hormuz reopening and the US 60-day general license (through 21 Aug). The tail that drove the hawkish Fed has been pulled — the single most important macro cross-current right now.

**5. UK politics — Starmer out.** PM Keir Starmer resigned 22 Jun after a Labour leadership crisis; Greater Manchester mayor **Andy Burnham** is the favourite to succeed him — Britain's 7th PM in 10 years. Gilt 10y near an 18-yr-high ~4.81% carries a fresh fiscal/political risk premium; sterling soft. A live, idiosyncratic UK risk for the desk.

**6. Dollar at a 1-yr high; EUR/USD at 2026 lows.** DXY 101.38, EUR/USD 1.1381 — rate-differential driven. The euro leg also reflects a barely-out-of-recession eurozone (composite PMI 49.5).

**7. Gold breaks $4,150.** Spot ~$4,129 (−1.5%), multi-week slide as firm real yields + risk-on drain the haven bid, outweighing the structural CB/EM buyer. Silver −2% to ~$64.

**8. Copper coils pre-Section 232.** COMEX ~$13.1–13.4k/t, holding gains (record $6.65/lb on 13 May) into the **30 Jun** Commerce refined-copper update (phased 15%→30% duty floated). Structural ~600kt 2026 deficit underpins.

## 4. Desk Angle — flows, vol, positioning

- **Risk is skewed to the AI-memory complex, both ways, into Micron.** The crowded long was the casualty Mon–Tue; the overnight bounce shows dip-demand intact but conviction thin. Expect elevated single-name and index vol around tonight's guide — and watch the *reaction* more than the number: a beat that doesn't hold is the signal a top is forming. The Dow-vs-Nasdaq split says the move so far is rotation, not de-grossing — that's the bullish read, but it flips fast if Micron disappoints and value can't absorb the outflow.
- **Rates: bear-flattener.** Front-end pinned by the hawkish dots (2y ~4.20%), long-end lagging (10y ~4.51%). The pain trade is a *dovish* surprise (soft PCE / dovish Fedspeak) that bull-steepens — under-owned given the consensus crouch.
- **FX: long USD is the consensus, and it's working.** DXY 1-yr high. USD/JPY ~160 is the asymmetric one — carry alive but you're short a lottery ticket on MoF intervention. EUR weak on growth; GBP carries a domestic political tail.
- **Gold:** crowded long still bleeding; dip-buyers eyed $4,050–4,150. A dovish oil-driven Fed re-think is the catalyst that snaps the real-yield headwind — not here yet.
