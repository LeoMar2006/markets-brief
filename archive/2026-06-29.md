## 3. What's Moving Markets

**1. OpenAI IPO delay — the AI-monetisation scare rolls into a new week.** NYT/Bloomberg (25–26 Jun): OpenAI is leaning to **delay its IPO to 2027** rather than price below Altman's insisted **$1tn** valuation; advisers offered a list-now-cheaper vs. wait-for-$1tn choice and Altman called a cut a "nonstarter." Context: ~$122bn raised at an **$852bn** valuation in early 2026; confidential S-1 filed 8 Jun. The read-through that hit the tape: the flagship AI franchise can't yet clear $1tn and is bleeding cash, so the market is discounting the **entire capex chain** (memory/GPU/power/hyperscaler). Note the *demand* leg is still intact (Micron HBM sold out into 2027); the broken leg is **return on capex**. Watch for any funding/valuation backstop — that would reverse sentiment fast.

**2. SoftBank −12% / Asia tech bears the brunt.** SoftBank shed ~$38bn in a single Friday session (−14% intraday), the sharpest drop in three months, as the biggest OpenAI backer (~$65bn stake by October). It remains the cleanest real-time proxy for AI-bubble anxiety — watch it and SK Hynix/Samsung into our open.

**3. US–Iran ceasefire fraying — and oil barely cares.** The 17 Jun MoU/ceasefire is under strain: Iran struck a commercial vessel (the *Ever Lovely*) Thursday, the US hit Iranian missile/drone/radar sites, and Iran fired at US bases in Kuwait/Bahrain over the weekend; both sides trade blame. **Yet Brent only nudged back to ~$70 from four-month lows.** That non-reaction is the story: with Hormuz transit recovered to ~75% of pre-war and Saudi loading at Ras Tanura, the market is treating the flare-up as noise against a structurally re-supplied balance. Asymmetric tail, though — a genuine Hormuz re-closure is not in the price.

**4. Quarter-/H1-end mechanics (Tue 30 Jun).** After a −3%-ish June for the S&P and a brutal month for AI/memory, month- and quarter-end rebalancing could produce a **mechanical equity-buy / bond-sell** impulse (pensions topping up underweight equities). Treat any sharp bounce in beaten-up tech this week as potentially flow-driven, not a fundamental all-clear.

**5. Copper into the 232 deadline (Tue).** COMEX consolidating near records (~$6.0/lb, ~$13.3k/t) ahead of Commerce's refined-copper update to the President. A ~600kt 2026 deficit (AI data-centres, grid, EV) underpins; a phased-duty confirmation would be the bullish catalyst, a punt or soft language the disappointment.

**6. Gold defends $4,000; silver leads.** Gold ~$4,064, a second up-day, holding the $4k line that broke and reclaimed last week after in-line May PCE pulled the faster-Fed tail. Silver ~$59 outperforming (G/S ~68.8). The "crowded-long under pressure" thesis is intact but a **low may be forming** while $4k holds.

**7. UK succession smoother; gilts firmer.** Gilt 10y eased to ~4.78% as Starmer's exit and a likely **Burnham coronation** (Streeting standing aside; noms 9–16 Jul, new leader by ~29 Aug) reduced near-term political risk, helped by soft UK PMIs.
