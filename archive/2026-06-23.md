**Oil is the swing factor.** The weekend's Hormuz scare has fully unwound. Physically, Iran shipped **>30m barrels in the past week** (highest since the conflict began) and cut prices to China, while Gulf flows normalised. Brent at ~$74 is back near early-March lows. This matters far beyond energy: it is the disinflationary release valve that complicates the Fed's freshly-hawkish stance.

## 3. What's Moving Markets

1. **US lifts Iran oil sanctions for 60 days.** The headline that re-rated energy and risk. A genuine de-escalation, but note the structure: a *temporary* waiver to 21 Aug, contingent on Iranian behaviour (Hormuz transit, IAEA access). It removes the tail but installs an August expiry as a fresh calendar risk. Brent -several %; this is disinflationary and risk-positive at the margin.

2. **Alphabet's AI brain-drain (-7%).** Two foundational researchers gone in a week to OpenAI and Anthropic. The market is asking whether Google's research edge — and the justification for ~$190bn of capex — is leaking. Idiosyncratic, but it dented the whole AI-mega-cap complex.

3. **SpaceX (SPCX) -16%, three down days.** The post-IPO digestion thesis (>$2tn cap, ~$75bn of capital absorbed, no S&P-inclusion profitability yet) is playing out. A drag on marginal risk appetite and a momentum-unwind signal.

4. **Hawkish-hold Fed vs a deflating oil impulse.** The 17 Jun dots flipped to a 2026 *hike* and lifted the PCE forecast to 3.6%, partly on an oil/inflation impulse that has since rolled over hard. Deutsche Bank and BofA still flag a September hike; the front end (2y ~4.19%) and DXY (>1yr high) reflect it. The live question: does Waller today, and PCE Thursday, validate the hawkishness — or does cheaper oil open a dovish re-think?

5. **Gold breaks ~$4,150 (-1.4%).** Firm dollar and real yields, plus risk-on draining the haven bid, outweigh the structural CB/EM story. Third-plus weekly decline; watch whether dip-buyers re-engage toward $4,050–4,150.

6. **Copper — structural-deficit clock ticking to 30 June.** Commerce's Section 232 update on refined copper is due **30 Jun**; COMEX inventories have ballooned >550% (to ~577k tons) on pre-positioning, COMEX 3-month hit a record ~$6.65/lb in May. The arbitrage and the deficit (forecasts of a ~600kt 2026 shortfall) keep the complex tense into the deadline.

7. **Crypto stabilises with risk-on.** BTC bounced back toward $65k on the Iran de-escalation after a weak first half of June; ETH ~$1,750. Still a cleaner risk-appetite tell than AI-led equity resilience — and it caught a bid as the dollar held firm, worth watching for divergence.

8. **Euro-area growth in focus.** With services in outright contraction (46.4), today's flash PMIs test whether the ECB's "June hike was the last" call rests on a softening economy as much as on oil disinflation.

## 4. Desk Angle — flows, vol, positioning

- **The central tension to trade:** a *hawkish* Fed (hawkish-hold, 2026-hike dots, PCE forecast 3.6%) layered over a *collapsing* oil/inflation impulse. The hawkish dots were partly built on an Iran premium that has now deflated. If Waller leans into the tilt today and PCE prints hot Thursday, the front-end/DXY/short-gold trade extends. But May PCE is **backward-looking** — it captures the oil-spike window — and with Brent now ~$74 the forward impulse is fading; a hot print may be the *last* hot print. Skew is toward a dovish re-think being underpriced into July.
- **Positioning:** crowded AI mega-cap long is the vulnerability — yesterday's GOOGL/SPCX air-pocket and the rotation into industrials show how one-sided the book had become. A clean unwind would hit Nasdaq breadth and crypto together.
- **Vol:** energy vol should bleed lower as the Hormuz tail is priced out (own a little, cheap, for the August-expiry waiver risk). Rates/FX vol stays bid into Waller and PCE. Equity vol cheap relative to single-name dispersion (GOOGL, SPCX, MU into the print).
- **Carry:** USD/JPY ~160 sits on the MoF intervention line — carry alive but asymmetric; a risk-off AI unwind plus any verbal intervention is the squeeze risk.
- **Where risk is skewed:** dollar long and gold short are consensus and crowded; the contrarian risk is a dovish oil-driven Fed re-think. Long cyclicals/short crowded-AI mega-cap is the cleaner expression of yesterday's rotation than fading the index.
