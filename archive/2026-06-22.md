## 3. What's Moving Markets

1. **Iran / Hormuz round-trip (top story).** Friday's postponement of the Bürgenstock technical talks (Vance pulled out) and a weekend IRGC declaration that Hormuz is "closed" — citing a US breach of the post-war MoU and renewed Israel–Hezbollah fire in southern Lebanon — briefly resurrected the war premium. By Monday the parties were back in high-level talks and oil handed back its gains. The honest read: the interim framework is fragile and reversible, but neither side looks to want a full rupture, and physical flows have kept moving. Expect more of this two-way chop, not a clean trend.

2. **The Warsh hawkish-hold still dominates rates and FX.** The 17 Jun hold (3.50–3.75%, 12–0) came with a dot-plot flip to a 2026 hike, a 2026 PCE forecast hiked to 3.6% from 2.7%, and dropped easing language. Markets now price ~67% odds of a September hike (intraday Thu) and the DXY has punched to a one-year high. The tension worth holding in your head: those hawkish dots leaned partly on an oil/inflation impulse that just round-tripped — if PCE cools and oil settles, the door to a dovish walk-back opens.

3. **Gold's third down-week.** ~$4,150 and pressured by the dollar/front-end, yet the weekend Iran scare shows the structural CB/EM and geopolitical bid is still there under the surface. JPM's ~$6,000 Q4 average call frames this as a correction within a bull market rather than a top — but a crowded long into rising real yields is vulnerable near term.

4. **Copper grinds near records.** Cochilco lifting its 2026 forecast to $5.55/lb crystallises the structural-deficit story (TC/RCs near zero, AI data-centre/grid/EV demand). The **30 Jun US tariff review** keeps the US-vs-ex-US dislocation alive. FCX's recent 12% air-pocket is a reminder the equity expression is high-beta and headline-sensitive.

5. **Micron as the AI tell.** Wednesday's print (rev +276% y/y) is the cleanest test of whether the memory/AI capex cycle that's holding up the Nasdaq is still accelerating. A beat-and-raise extends the equity-resilience-vs-everything-else divergence; a disappointment would force equities to confront what crypto and gold are already pricing.

6. **Crypto keeps flashing caution.** BTC ~$62–63k, ETH ~$1,700 — pressured by ETF outflows, DXY >100, higher-for-longer, and treasury-company selling. As a duration-free, sentiment-pure asset it is arguably the most honest gauge of risk appetite on the desk, and it disagrees with the AI-equity calm.

7. **Japan: record Nikkei, USD/JPY on the line.** Nikkei near 71,700 on AI/exporters and a weak yen; USD/JPY ~160 sits on the MoF intervention threshold. Carry is alive but the asymmetry (capped upside, intervention tail) is worsening.
